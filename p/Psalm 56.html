Psalm 56

1. Be gracious to me, O God, for man tramples on me; all day long an attacker oppresses me;

2. my enemies trample on me all day long, for many attack me proudly.

3. When I am afraid, I put my trust in you.

4. In God, whose word I praise, in God I trust; I shall not be afraid. What can flesh do to me?

5. All day long they injure my cause; all their thoughts are against me for evil.

6. They stir up strife, they lurk; they watch my steps, as they have waited for my life.

7. For their crime will they escape? In wrath cast down the peoples, O God!

8. You have kept count of my tossings; put my tears in your bottle. Are they not in your book?

9. Then my enemies will turn back in the day when I call. This I know, that God is for me.

10. In God, whose word I praise, in the Lord, whose word I praise,

11. in God I trust; I shall not be afraid. What can man do to me?

12. I must perform my vows to you, O God; I will render thank offerings to you.

13. For you have delivered my soul from death, yes, my feet from falling, that I may walk before God in the light of life.

Commentary

Psalm 56 is a prayer of David in a time of distress, expressing his trust in God's protection and deliverance. It highlights his confidence in God's faithfulness and recounts his enemies' attacks.

David begins by appealing to God for His grace, as he faces oppression and attack from his enemies throughout the day. He acknowledges the proud and numerous assailants who trample upon him and are against him.

In times of fear, David declares his trust in God, praising His word and expressing his confidence that he will not be afraid. He questions what harm mere mortals can do to him when he has placed his trust in the Almighty.

David describes how his enemies constantly injure his cause and plot against him. They stir up strife and watch his every step, eagerly waiting for an opportunity to take his life. He appeals to God's justice, asking whether his enemies will escape punishment and expressing his desire for divine wrath to be cast upon them.

David recognizes God's care for him, acknowledging that God has kept count of his sleepless nights and collected his tears in a bottle, symbolizing God's attentiveness to his distress. He believes that when he calls upon God, his enemies will retreat, affirming his trust that God is on his side. David continues to praise God's word and expresses his confidence in the Lord's protection.

David vows to fulfill his obligations to God, promising to offer thanksgiving and praise offerings. He acknowledges that God has delivered his soul from death and kept his feet from stumbling, enabling him to walk before God in the light of life.

Psalm 56 showcases David's unwavering trust in God amidst adversity. It illustrates the intimate relationship between the psalmist and God, as he seeks God's grace, acknowledges God's care, and expresses confidence in His deliverance. The psalm encourages believers to place their trust in God, even when facing persecution and opposition, and to find comfort in His faithful presence and protection.

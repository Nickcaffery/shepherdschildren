Psalm 124

1. If it had not been the Lord who was on our side— let Israel now say—

2. if it had not been the Lord who was on our side when people rose up against us,

3. then they would have swallowed us up alive, when their anger was kindled against us;

4. then the flood would have swept us away, the torrent would have gone over us;

5. then over us would have gone the raging waters.

6. Blessed be the Lord, who has not given us as prey to their teeth!

7. We have escaped like a bird from the snare of the fowlers; the snare is broken, and we have escaped!

8. Our help is in the name of the Lord, who made heaven and earth.

Commentary

Psalm 124 is a psalm of thanksgiving and praise, acknowledging the Lord's deliverance and protection. The psalmist declares that if it had not been for the Lord who was on their side, they would have faced destruction and been overwhelmed by their enemies.

The psalmist calls upon Israel to acknowledge and affirm that it is the Lord who has been their support and defender. They reflect on a specific occasion when people rose up against them with anger and hostility. The psalmist acknowledges that if it had not been for the Lord's intervention, they would have been swallowed up alive and swept away by a flood or raging waters. The psalmist vividly portrays the peril they faced and the imminent danger they were spared from.

In response to this deliverance, the psalmist blesses and praises the Lord. They acknowledge the Lord's intervention, expressing gratitude for not being given as prey to their enemies. They use the imagery of a bird escaping from a snare, emphasizing the liberation and freedom they have experienced. The psalmist rejoices in the breaking of the snare and the escape that followed.

The psalm concludes with an affirmation of trust in the Lord. The psalmist declares that their help comes from the name of the Lord, the one who made heaven and earth. They recognize the Lord as the ultimate source of their deliverance, emphasizing His power and sovereignty over all creation.

Psalm 124 serves as a testimony of the Lord's protection and deliverance. It highlights the psalmist's gratitude for being spared from destruction and overwhelmed by their enemies. The psalmist acknowledges the Lord as their source of help and expresses reliance on His name. The psalm invites the reader to reflect on the Lord's faithfulness and provision in times of adversity and to offer praise and thanksgiving for His deliverance.

Psalm 117

1. Praise the Lord, all you nations; extol him, all you peoples.

2. For great is his steadfast love toward us, and the faithfulness of the Lord endures forever. Praise the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 117 is the shortest chapter in the Book of Psalms, consisting of only two verses. Despite its brevity, it carries a powerful message of universal praise and the enduring love and faithfulness of the Lord.

The psalmist begins by calling all nations and peoples to praise the Lord. It is an invitation for everyone, regardless of their nationality or background, to extol and worship the Lord. The psalmist recognizes that the Lord is deserving of praise from all humanity.

The psalmist highlights two key attributes of the Lord: His great steadfast love and His enduring faithfulness. The steadfast love of the Lord refers to His unwavering, loyal, and unfailing love towards His people. It is a love that persists and endures throughout all circumstances. The psalmist also affirms the everlasting faithfulness of the Lord, emphasizing His reliability, trustworthiness, and commitment to His promises.

The psalm concludes with a resounding call to praise the Lord. It is a reminder to acknowledge and honor the Lord for His steadfast love and enduring faithfulness.

Psalm 117 serves as a universal call to praise the Lord, inviting all nations and peoples to recognize His greatness and offer their adoration. It underscores the unchanging and everlasting nature of God's love and faithfulness. Despite its brevity, this psalm carries a profound message of the Lord's all-encompassing love and faithfulness, deserving the praise of all humanity.

Psalm 31

1. In you, O Lord, do I take refuge; let me never be put to shame; in your righteousness deliver me!

2. Incline your ear to me; rescue me speedily! Be a rock of refuge for me, a strong fortress to save me!

3. For you are my rock and my fortress; and for your name's sake you lead me and guide me;

4. you take me out of the net they have hidden for me, for you are my refuge.

5. Into your hand I commit my spirit; you have redeemed me, O Lord, faithful God.

6. I hate those who pay regard to worthless idols, but I trust in the Lord.

7. I will rejoice and be glad in your steadfast love, because you have seen my affliction; you have known the distress of my soul,

8. and you have not delivered me into the hand of the enemy; you have set my feet in a broad place.

9. Be gracious to me, O Lord, for I am in distress; my eye is wasted from grief; my soul and my body also.

10. For my life is spent with sorrow, and my years with sighing; my strength fails because of my iniquity, and my bones waste away.

11. Because of all my adversaries I have become a reproach, especially to my neighbors, and an object of dread to my acquaintances; those who see me in the street flee from me.

12. I have been forgotten like one who is dead; I have become like a broken vessel.

13. For I hear the whispering of many— terror on every side!— as they scheme together against me, as they plot to take my life.

14. But I trust in you, O Lord; I say, "You are my God."

15. My times are in your hand; rescue me from the hand of my enemies and from my persecutors!

16. Make your face shine on your servant; save me in your steadfast love!

17. O Lord, let me not be put to shame, for I call upon you; let the wicked be put to shame; let them go silently to Sheol.

18. Let the lying lips be mute, which speak insolently against the righteous in pride and contempt.

19. Oh, how abundant is your goodness, which you have stored up for those who fear you and worked for those who take refuge in you, in the sight of the children of mankind!

20. In the cover of your presence you hide them from the plots of men; you store them in your shelter from the strife of tongues.

21. Blessed be the Lord, for he has wondrously shown his steadfast love to me when I was in a besieged city.

22. I had said in my alarm, "I am cut off from your sight." But you heard the voice of my pleas for mercy when I cried to you for help.

23. Love the Lord, all you his saints! The Lord preserves the faithful but abundantly repays the one who acts in pride.

24. Be strong, and let your heart take courage, all you who wait for the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 31 is a psalm of trust and reliance on the Lord in the face of distress and opposition. The psalmist takes refuge in God, seeking deliverance and protection from their enemies. They express their trust in God's steadfast love and declare their commitment to Him.

The psalmist begins by acknowledging God as their refuge and rock of strength. They implore God to rescue them quickly and be a fortress of salvation. They recognize God as their rock and fortress, leading and guiding them for the sake of His name. They trust in God as their refuge, acknowledging His redemption and faithfulness.

The psalmist expresses their rejection of worthless idols and their trust in the Lord. They rejoice in God's steadfast love because He has seen their affliction and known the distress of their soul. They acknowledge that God has not delivered them into the hands of their enemies but has set their feet in a broad place.

In the midst of distress and suffering, the psalmist pleads for God's graciousness, expressing the toll it has taken on their body and spirit. They describe the reproach, dread, and isolation they have experienced from adversaries. They feel forgotten and broken, facing threats on every side. Despite these challenges, they reaffirm their trust in the Lord as their God.

The psalmist places their times and life in God's hands, appealing for rescue from enemies and persecutors. They seek the shining of God's face and salvation through His steadfast love. They call upon the Lord, asking not to be put to shame, and pray for the wicked to be silenced and shamed.

The psalmist marvels at the goodness of God, stored up for those who fear Him and take refuge in Him. They acknowledge God's protection and shelter from the schemes and strife of others.

The psalmist blesses the Lord for His wondrous display of steadfast love, even in the midst of being in a besieged city. They testify that God heard their pleas for mercy and cries for help.

The psalm concludes with an exhortation to all the Lord's saints to love Him. The Lord preserves the faithful but repays the proud. The psalmist encourages strength and courage for those who wait for the Lord.

Psalm 31 is a powerful expression of trust and reliance on God amidst adversity. It demonstrates the psalmist's deep dependence on God as their refuge and the source of their deliverance. The psalmist's words of praise, pleas for mercy, and declarations of trust serve as an inspiration and encouragement for all who face trials and challenges.

Psalm 127

1. Unless the Lord builds the house, those who build it labor in vain. Unless the Lord watches over the city, the watchman stays awake in vain.

2. It is in vain that you rise up early and go late to rest, eating the bread of anxious toil; for he gives to his beloved sleep.

3. Behold, children are a heritage from the Lord, the fruit of the womb a reward.

4. Like arrows in the hand of a warrior are the children of one's youth.

5. Blessed is the man who fills his quiver with them! He shall not be put to shame when he speaks with his enemies in the gate.

Commentary

Psalm 127 is a psalm that addresses the importance of relying on the Lord in various aspects of life, particularly in building and protecting a house or city. It also highlights the value and blessing of children as a heritage from the Lord.

The psalm begins by emphasizing the futility of human efforts without the Lord's involvement. Unless the Lord is the one building the house or watching over the city, all human labor and vigilance will be in vain. The psalmist emphasizes the need to acknowledge God's sovereignty and trust in His providence.

The psalmist highlights the futility of anxious toil, where individuals rise up early and go to bed late, consumed by their work and worries. The psalmist contrasts this with the assurance that the Lord gives sleep to His beloved. It signifies the rest and peace that come from entrusting one's efforts and concerns to God.

The psalm then shifts its focus to the blessing and value of children. Children are described as a heritage from the Lord, a reward, and compared to arrows in the hand of a warrior. Like arrows in the hand of a skilled archer, children are seen as valuable assets, providing strength, protection, and influence. The psalmist pronounces blessings upon those who have a full quiver of children, indicating the honor and security they will experience when engaging with their enemies in the gate, symbolizing places of judgment and authority.

Psalm 127 conveys the message of acknowledging God's sovereignty and relying on Him in various aspects of life. It emphasizes the futility of human efforts without the Lord's involvement and the importance of finding rest and peace in Him. The psalm also highlights the value and blessing of children as a heritage from the Lord, emphasizing their significance in providing strength, protection, and influence. It encourages readers to trust in the Lord's guidance and blessings in building their lives and families.

Psalm 146

1. Praise the Lord! Praise the Lord, O my soul!

2. I will praise the Lord as long as I live; I will sing praises to my God while I have my being.

3. Put not your trust in princes, in a son of man, in whom there is no salvation.

4. When his breath departs, he returns to the earth; on that very day his plans perish.

5. Blessed is he whose help is the God of Jacob, whose hope is in the Lord his God,

6. who made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that is in them, who keeps faith forever;

7. who executes justice for the oppressed, who gives food to the hungry. The Lord sets the prisoners free;

8. the Lord opens the eyes of the blind. The Lord lifts up those who are bowed down; the Lord loves the righteous.

9. The Lord watches over the sojourners; he upholds the widow and the fatherless, but the way of the wicked he brings to ruin.

10. The Lord will reign forever, your God, O Zion, to all generations. Praise the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 146 is a psalm of praise, exalting the Lord and encouraging trust in Him rather than in earthly powers. The psalmist begins by urging their soul to praise the Lord, vowing to praise Him as long as they live and sing praises to God throughout their existence.

The psalmist warns against putting trust in human princes or relying on the power and promises of mortal beings. They highlight the temporary nature of human life, emphasizing that when a person's breath departs, their plans perish.

The psalmist declares the blessedness of those whose help and hope are in the God of Jacob, the Lord. They acknowledge God as the creator of heaven and earth, the sea, and everything in them. They praise God for His eternal faithfulness, His execution of justice for the oppressed, and His provision for the hungry.

The psalmist recognizes the Lord's acts of liberation and compassion. The Lord sets prisoners free, opens the eyes of the blind, lifts up those who are bowed down, and shows love to the righteous. The psalmist highlights God's care for sojourners, widows, and orphans, affirming that the Lord watches over and upholds them. Conversely, the psalmist declares that the wicked will face ruin.

The psalm concludes by proclaiming that the Lord will reign forever. The psalmist addresses Zion, symbolizing God's people, and assures that the Lord will be their God throughout all generations. The psalmist ends by urging praise to the Lord.

Psalm 146 is a psalm of praise, emphasizing trust in the eternal and compassionate God over the transient and limited power of human beings. The psalmist celebrates God's acts of justice, provision, liberation, and care for the vulnerable. They affirm God's eternal reign and call for continual praise to the Lord.

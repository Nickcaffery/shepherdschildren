Psalm 113

1. Praise the Lord! Praise, O servants of the Lord, praise the name of the Lord!

2. Blessed be the name of the Lord from this time forth and forevermore!

3. From the rising of the sun to its setting, the name of the Lord is to be praised!

4. The Lord is high above all nations, and his glory above the heavens!

5. Who is like the Lord our God, who is seated on high,

6. who looks far down on the heavens and the earth?

7. He raises the poor from the dust and lifts the needy from the ash heap,

8. to make them sit with princes, with the princes of his people.

9. He gives the barren woman a home, making her the joyous mother of children. Praise the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 113 is a psalm of praise that exalts the name and greatness of the Lord. It calls for the servants of the Lord to praise His name from now and forever.

The psalmist declares the blessedness of the Lord's name, emphasizing that it should be praised from the rising to the setting of the sun. The name of the Lord is to be exalted and honored throughout all time.

The psalmist acknowledges the supreme position of the Lord, as He is high above all nations and His glory surpasses the heavens. They marvel at the uniqueness and unmatched greatness of the Lord our God, who is seated on high and has a view over the entire heavens and earth.

The psalmist highlights the compassion and power of the Lord. They describe how the Lord raises the poor from the dust and lifts the needy from the ash heap, elevating them to sit with princes, the nobles of His people. The Lord brings about transformation, granting the barren woman a home and the joy of becoming a mother to children.

The psalm concludes with a call to praise the Lord once again, expressing gratitude and adoration for His merciful actions and mighty deeds.

Psalm 113 celebrates the greatness and compassion of the Lord, who is exalted above all nations. It encourages the servants of the Lord to praise His name and magnify His works. The psalm highlights God's ability to lift up the humble and bring joy to the needy, emphasizing His sovereignty and care for the vulnerable.

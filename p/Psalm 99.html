Psalm 99

1. The Lord reigns; let the peoples tremble! He sits enthroned upon the cherubim; let the earth quake!

2. The Lord is great in Zion; he is exalted over all the peoples.

3. Let them praise your great and awesome name! Holy is he!

4. The King in his might loves justice. You have established equity; you have executed justice and righteousness in Jacob.

5. Exalt the Lord our God; worship at his footstool! Holy is he!

6. Moses and Aaron were among his priests, Samuel also was among those who called upon his name. They called to the Lord, and he answered them.

7. In the pillar of the cloud he spoke to them; they kept his testimonies and the statute that he gave them.

8. O Lord our God, you answered them; you were a forgiving God to them, but an avenger of their wrongdoings.

9. Exalt the Lord our God, and worship at his holy mountain; for the Lord our God is holy!

Commentary

Psalm 99 is a psalm that declares the sovereignty and holiness of the Lord. It invites people to praise and worship Him, acknowledging His justice, righteousness, and forgiveness.

The psalmist begins by proclaiming that the Lord reigns, causing the peoples to tremble and the earth to quake. They describe Him as being enthroned upon the cherubim and being great and exalted over all the peoples. The psalmist calls for the praise of His great and awesome name, affirming His holiness.

The psalmist acknowledges the Lord's love for justice and His establishment of equity. They recognize that He executes justice and righteousness in Jacob (referring to the nation of Israel). The psalmist exalts the Lord, urging worship at His footstool and declaring His holiness.

The psalmist recalls the faithfulness of Moses, Aaron, and Samuel as they served as priests and called upon the name of the Lord. They highlight how the Lord answered their prayers and spoke to them in the pillar of the cloud. They emphasize that these individuals kept His testimonies and the statutes He gave them.

The psalmist acknowledges that the Lord answered their prayers and forgave them, but also acted as an avenger of their wrongdoings. They reiterate the call to exalt the Lord our God and worship at His holy mountain, for He is holy.

Psalm 99 highlights the reign, holiness, and justice of the Lord. It calls for praise and worship, recognizing His greatness and exaltation over all. The psalm acknowledges the faithful figures of Moses, Aaron, and Samuel and their interaction with the Lord. It emphasizes the forgiveness and avenging nature of God and concludes with an exhortation to exalt and worship the Lord, affirming His holiness.

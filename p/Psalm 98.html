Psalm 98

1. Oh sing to the Lord a new song, for he has done marvelous things! His right hand and his holy arm have worked salvation for him.

2. The Lord has made known his salvation; he has revealed his righteousness in the sight of the nations.

3. He has remembered his steadfast love and faithfulness to the house of Israel. All the ends of the earth have seen the salvation of our God.

4. Make a joyful noise to the Lord, all the earth; break forth into joyous song and sing praises!

5. Sing praises to the Lord with the lyre, with the lyre and the sound of melody!

6. With trumpets and the sound of the horn make a joyful noise before the King, the Lord!

7. Let the sea roar, and all that fills it; the world and those who dwell in it!

8. Let the rivers clap their hands; let the hills sing for joy together

9. before the Lord, for he comes to judge the earth. He will judge the world with righteousness, and the peoples with equity.

Commentary

Psalm 98 is a psalm of praise and celebration, inviting all the earth to sing and make joyful noise to the Lord. It focuses on the marvelous salvation and righteousness of God and anticipates His coming to judge the earth.

The psalmist begins by calling for a new song to be sung to the Lord, celebrating the marvelous things He has done. They acknowledge that God's right hand and holy arm have worked salvation for Him. The Lord has made known His salvation and revealed His righteousness to the nations. He has remembered His steadfast love and faithfulness to the house of Israel, and the salvation of God has been witnessed by all the ends of the earth.

The psalmist urges all the earth to make a joyful noise to the Lord and break forth into joyous songs of praise. They specifically mention using instruments such as the lyre, trumpets, and the sound of the horn to accompany the joyful noise. The psalmist calls for the sea to roar, the world and its inhabitants to join in praising the Lord. They encourage the rivers to clap their hands and the hills to sing for joy together before the King, the Lord.

The psalmist anticipates the coming of the Lord to judge the earth. They affirm that He will judge the world with righteousness and the peoples with equity. This judgment is seen as a cause for rejoicing and celebration.

Psalm 98 emphasizes the call to praise and celebrate the marvelous salvation and righteousness of the Lord. It invites all the earth, along with its creatures and natural elements, to join in joyful singing and making a joyful noise before the King. The psalm anticipates the future judgment of the Lord, which is regarded as an occasion for rejoicing and the establishment of righteousness and equity.

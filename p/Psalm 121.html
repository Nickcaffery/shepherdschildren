Psalm 121

1. I lift up my eyes to the hills. From where does my help come?

2. My help comes from the Lord, who made heaven and earth.

3. He will not let your foot be moved; he who keeps you will not slumber.

4. Behold, he who keeps Israel will neither slumber nor sleep.

5. The Lord is your keeper; the Lord is your shade on your right hand.

6. The sun shall not strike you by day, nor the moon by night.

7. The Lord will keep you from all evil; he will keep your life.

8. The Lord will keep your going out and your coming in from this time forth and forevermore.

Commentary

Psalm 121 is a comforting psalm known as the "Song of Ascents." It is often recited or sung by individuals seeking divine help, assurance, and protection. The psalmist acknowledges their dependence on the Lord and affirms His continuous watchfulness and care.

The psalmist begins by looking up to the hills and posing a rhetorical question: "From where does my help come?" They acknowledge that their help comes from the Lord, the Creator of heaven and earth. The psalmist establishes that their trust and reliance are in the Lord alone.

The psalmist assures the reader that the Lord will not allow their foot to be moved or stumble. The Lord, who keeps and watches over them, will remain alert and attentive without slumbering or sleeping. This emphasizes the unwavering presence and protection of the Lord.

The psalmist depicts the Lord as a keeper and a shade on the right hand, providing refuge and protection from the scorching sun and the potential dangers that arise day or night. The psalmist reassures that no harm will come upon the individual under the Lord's watchful care.

The psalmist declares that the Lord will keep the person from all evil, safeguarding their life. The Lord is seen as the protector of every aspect of their existence. The psalmist emphasizes that the Lord will guard their going out and their coming in, signifying the Lord's continuous presence and guidance throughout their entire life.

The psalm concludes by affirming that the Lord's keeping and protection extend from that time forth and forevermore. It establishes the eternal nature of the Lord's watchfulness and care, assuring the reader of the enduring presence and faithfulness of God.

Psalm 121 offers comfort and reassurance to those who look to the Lord for help and protection. It conveys the trust and reliance placed in the Lord, who is ever-vigilant and provides unwavering support. The psalm reminds the reader of the Lord's constant presence and watchful care, bringing comfort and confidence in times of need.

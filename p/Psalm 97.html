Psalm 97

1. The Lord reigns, let the earth rejoice; let the many coastlands be glad!

2. Clouds and thick darkness are all around him; righteousness and justice are the foundation of his throne.

3. Fire goes before him and burns up his adversaries all around.

4. His lightnings light up the world; the earth sees and trembles.

5. The mountains melt like wax before the Lord, before the Lord of all the earth.

6. The heavens proclaim his righteousness, and all the peoples see his glory.

7. All worshipers of images are put to shame, who make their boast in worthless idols; worship him, all you gods!

8. Zion hears and is glad, and the daughters of Judah rejoice, because of your judgments, O Lord.

9. For you, O Lord, are most high over all the earth; you are exalted far above all gods.

10. O you who love the Lord, hate evil! He preserves the lives of his saints; he delivers them from the hand of the wicked.

11. Light is sown for the righteous, and joy for the upright in heart.

12. Rejoice in the Lord, O you righteous, and give thanks to his holy name!

Commentary

Psalm 97 is a psalm that exalts the reign and righteousness of the Lord. It emphasizes His sovereignty, power, and the call to worship and rejoice in His judgments.

The psalmist begins by declaring that the Lord reigns, inviting the earth to rejoice and the coastlands to be glad. They describe the presence of clouds and thick darkness surrounding God, while righteousness and justice form the foundation of His throne. Fire goes before Him, consuming His adversaries, and His lightnings illuminate the world, causing the earth to tremble. The psalmist depicts the mountains melting like wax before the Lord, acknowledging His supreme authority over all the earth.

The psalmist proclaims that the heavens declare God's righteousness, and all people witness His glory. They highlight the shame and futility of worshipers of idols who boast in worthless images. The psalmist calls for worship of the Lord, exhorting all gods to acknowledge His supremacy.

The psalmist depicts Zion (Jerusalem) and the daughters of Judah rejoicing in the Lord's judgments. They acknowledge that the Lord is exalted far above all gods and holds dominion over the entire earth.

The psalmist instructs those who love the Lord to hate evil. They affirm that the Lord preserves the lives of His saints and delivers them from the hand of the wicked. Light is sown for the righteous, symbolizing blessings and prosperity, while joy is reserved for those with upright hearts.

The psalm concludes with an exhortation for the righteous to rejoice in the Lord and give thanks to His holy name.

Psalm 97 exalts the reign of the Lord and emphasizes His righteousness and justice. It proclaims His authority and power over all creation, causing the earth and mountains to tremble before Him. The psalm calls for worship and denounces the worship of idols. It invites the righteous to hate evil and find joy in God's presence. The psalm concludes with an exhortation to rejoice and give thanks to the Lord.

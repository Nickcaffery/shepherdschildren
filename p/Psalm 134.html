Psalm 134

1. Come, bless the Lord, all you servants of the Lord, who stand by night in the house of the Lord!

2. Lift up your hands to the holy place and bless the Lord!

3. May the Lord bless you from Zion, he who made heaven and earth!

Commentary

Psalm 134 is a short psalm that serves as a call to worship and a benediction. It addresses the servants of the Lord, specifically those who stand by night in the house of the Lord, likely referring to the priests or temple attendants. The psalm calls them to bless the Lord and lift up their hands in worship and adoration.

The psalm begins by summoning all the servants of the Lord to come and bless the Lord. It is an invitation to engage in worship and praise, acknowledging the greatness and holiness of the Lord. The psalmist recognizes the service of those who faithfully stand in the house of the Lord during the night, indicating their devotion and commitment to their sacred duties.

The psalmist instructs the worshippers to lift up their hands to the holy place, a gesture of surrender, reverence, and supplication. By lifting their hands, they demonstrate their recognition of the Lord's authority and their desire to offer Him praise and adoration.

In the final verse, the psalmist pronounces a benediction, expressing the desire for the Lord's blessings upon the worshippers. The psalmist acknowledges that it is the Lord who made heaven and earth, emphasizing His power and sovereignty over all creation. The psalmist calls upon the Lord to bless the worshippers from Zion, the symbolic dwelling place of God's presence.

Psalm 134 serves as a call to worship and a benediction to the servants of the Lord. It encourages the worshippers, particularly those who serve in the temple, to bless the Lord and offer Him their adoration. The psalmist acknowledges the Lord's authority and power as the Creator of heaven and earth and desires His blessings upon the worshippers. It invites readers to engage in worship and reminds them of the significance of acknowledging and praising the Lord's greatness.

Psalm 13

1. How long, O Lord? Will you forget me forever? How long will you hide your face from me?

2. How long must I take counsel in my soul and have sorrow in my heart all the day? How long shall my enemy be exalted over me?

3. Consider and answer me, O Lord my God; light up my eyes, lest I sleep the sleep of death,

4. lest my enemy say, "I have prevailed over him," lest my foes rejoice because I am shaken.

5. But I have trusted in your steadfast love; my heart shall rejoice in your salvation.

6. I will sing to the Lord, because he has dealt bountifully with me.

Commentary

Psalm 13 is a prayer of lament and trust in God's faithfulness, attributed to King David. It expresses the psalmist's deep anguish and longing for God's presence and intervention, while ultimately affirming trust in God's steadfast love and rejoicing in His salvation.

The psalm begins with a cry to the Lord, questioning how long God will forget the psalmist and hide His face. The psalmist expresses a sense of abandonment and feels as though God has turned away from him. The psalmist also acknowledges the prolonged sorrow and the exaltation of his enemy over him.

In verse 3, the psalmist pleads with the Lord to consider his situation and to answer him. The psalmist asks for God's intervention and for the light of His presence to shine upon him, as if to prevent him from falling into the sleep of death. The psalmist fears that if God does not act, his enemies will believe they have prevailed and will rejoice at his downfall.

However, in the midst of despair, the psalmist declares trust in God's steadfast love. Despite the circumstances, the psalmist's heart will rejoice in God's salvation. This statement reflects a shift in perspective, as the psalmist chooses to focus on God's faithfulness rather than the current troubles.

The psalm concludes with a declaration of praise and gratitude. The psalmist commits to singing to the Lord because of His abundant goodness and blessings. Although the psalmist may still be in a challenging situation, the psalmist affirms that God has dealt bountifully with him in the past.

Psalm 13 expresses the psalmist's raw emotions of distress and feeling abandoned by God. It portrays the psalmist's longing for God's intervention and the struggle to maintain trust in God's faithfulness. Ultimately, the psalmist chooses to cling to the steadfast love of God, finding hope in the assurance of His salvation and expressing gratitude for His past blessings.

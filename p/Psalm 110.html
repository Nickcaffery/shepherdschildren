Psalm 110

1. The Lord says to my Lord: "Sit at my right hand, until I make your enemies your footstool."

2. The Lord sends forth from Zion your mighty scepter. Rule in the midst of your enemies!

3. Your people will offer themselves freely on the day of your power, in holy garments; from the womb of the morning, the dew of your youth will be yours.

4. The Lord has sworn and will not change his mind, "You are a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek."

5. The Lord is at your right hand; he will shatter kings on the day of his wrath.

6. He will execute judgment among the nations, filling them with corpses; he will shatter chiefs over the wide earth.

7. He will drink from the brook by the way; therefore he will lift up his head.

Commentary

Psalm 110 is a Messianic psalm that speaks of the exalted status and authority of the Lord's anointed, who is addressed as "my Lord" by the psalmist. This psalm is often understood as pointing to the Messiah, Jesus Christ.

The psalm begins with a divine declaration, where the Lord speaks to the psalmist's Lord, inviting him to sit at His right hand. This position signifies a place of honor, authority, and power. The psalmist envisions the exaltation and enthronement of the Lord's anointed, who will be victorious over his enemies.

The Lord sends forth his mighty scepter from Zion, symbolizing the authority and dominion of the anointed one. The psalmist acknowledges that the Lord's anointed will rule in the midst of his enemies, demonstrating his power and sovereignty.

The psalmist describes the voluntary devotion of the people to the Lord's anointed on the day of his power. They offer themselves freely and wear holy garments, signifying their dedication and readiness to serve him. The imagery of the dew of youth from the womb of the morning represents the vitality and strength of the Lord's anointed.

The psalm highlights the eternal priesthood of the Lord's anointed, stating that he is a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek. This reference connects the Lord's anointed with the mysterious figure of Melchizedek, who was both a king and a priest.

The psalmist declares that the Lord is at the right hand of the Lord's anointed, emphasizing God's support, protection, and involvement in his victorious reign. The Lord will bring judgment upon the nations, shattering kings and chiefs, filling the earth with corpses. The psalmist envisions the Lord's anointed executing divine justice and displaying his power over the nations.

The psalm concludes with a vivid image of the Lord's anointed drinking from the brook by the way and then lifting up his head in triumph. This image symbolizes his satisfaction and victory over his enemies.

Psalm 110 points to the exalted status and authority of the Lord's anointed, who is envisioned as a powerful ruler and eternal priest. It speaks of his victory over enemies, his role in executing judgment, and his ultimate triumph. The psalm reflects the Messianic hope and foreshadows the coming of Jesus Christ as the fulfillment of these prophetic words.

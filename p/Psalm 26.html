Psalm 26

1. Vindicate me, O Lord, for I have walked in my integrity, and I have trusted in the Lord without wavering.

2. Prove me, O Lord, and try me; test my heart and my mind.

3. For your steadfast love is before my eyes, and I walk in your faithfulness.

4. I do not sit with men of falsehood, nor do I consort with hypocrites.

5. I hate the assembly of evildoers, and I will not sit with the wicked.

6. I wash my hands in innocence and go around your altar, O Lord,

7. proclaiming thanksgiving aloud, and telling all your wondrous deeds.

8. O Lord, I love the habitation of your house and the place where your glory dwells.

9. Do not sweep my soul away with sinners, nor my life with bloodthirsty men,

10. in whose hands are evil devices, and whose right hands are full of bribes.

11. But as for me, I shall walk in my integrity; redeem me, and be gracious to me.

12. My foot stands on level ground; in the great assembly I will bless the Lord.

Commentary

Psalm 26 is a prayer of the psalmist asking God to vindicate their integrity and protect them from the company of evildoers. The psalmist expresses their commitment to righteousness and their love for the presence of God.

The psalmist begins by appealing to God for vindication, proclaiming their own integrity and unwavering trust in the Lord. They invite God to examine and test their heart and mind, confident in His steadfast love and faithfulness.

The psalmist distances themselves from men of falsehood and hypocrites, expressing hatred towards evildoers and a refusal to associate with them. They affirm their commitment to righteousness.

The psalmist describes their ritual purity and devotion to God's house. They wash their hands in innocence and go around God's altar, proclaiming thanksgiving and recounting His wondrous deeds. The psalmist expresses love for the habitation of God's house and the place where His glory dwells.

The psalmist prays for God's protection, asking not to be swept away with sinners or bloodthirsty men who devise evil plans and engage in bribery. They declare their intention to walk in integrity and ask God for redemption and grace.

The psalm concludes with an expression of confidence and assurance. The psalmist declares that their foot stands on level ground, symbolizing stability and security. They express their commitment to bless the Lord in the great assembly, affirming their dedication to worship and praise.

Psalm 26 highlights the psalmist's commitment to integrity and righteousness. It emphasizes their desire to be separated from evildoers and their love for the presence of God. The psalmist appeals to God for vindication, protection, redemption, and grace. The psalm is a declaration of devotion to God and a plea for His favor and guidance.

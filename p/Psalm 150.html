Psalm 150

1. Praise the Lord! Praise God in his sanctuary; praise him in his mighty heavens!

2. Praise him for his mighty deeds; praise him according to his excellent greatness!

3. Praise him with trumpet sound; praise him with lute and harp!

4. Praise him with tambourine and dance; praise him with strings and pipe!

5. Praise him with sounding cymbals; praise him with loud clashing cymbals!

6. Let everything that has breath praise the Lord! Praise the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 150 is the final psalm in the Book of Psalms, and it serves as a grand conclusion and exhortation to praise the Lord. The psalmist begins by urging the praise of the Lord in His sanctuary and in the mighty heavens. They emphasize that God is deserving of praise for His mighty deeds and His excellent greatness.

The psalmist then provides a variety of instruments and forms of praise to be used in worshiping God. They mention the trumpet, lute, harp, tambourine, dance, strings, pipe, sounding cymbals, and loud clashing cymbals. The psalmist calls for a vibrant and joyful expression of praise using a diverse range of instruments and physical movements.

Finally, the psalmist concludes by proclaiming that everything that has breath should praise the Lord. This inclusive statement encompasses all living beings and emphasizes the universal call to worship and glorify God.

Psalm 150 serves as a triumphant finale to the Book of Psalms, declaring the praise and greatness of the Lord. It encourages the use of various instruments and forms of praise to honor God, and it calls upon all living creatures to join in worshiping the Lord. The psalm embodies the spirit of joyful adoration and invites all to participate in the celebration of God's glory.

Psalm 27

1. The Lord is my light and my salvation; whom shall I fear? The Lord is the stronghold of my life; of whom shall I be afraid?

2. When evildoers assail me to eat up my flesh, my adversaries and foes, it is they who stumble and fall.

3. Though an army encamp against me, my heart shall not fear; though war arise against me, yet I will be confident.

4. One thing have I asked of the Lord, that will I seek after: all the days of my life, to gaze upon the beauty of the Lord and to inquire in his temple.

5. For he will hide me in his shelter in the day of trouble; he will conceal me under the cover of his tent; he will lift me high upon a rock.

6. And now my head shall be lifted up above my enemies all around me, and I will offer in his tent sacrifices with shouts of joy; I will sing and make melody to the Lord.

7. Hear, O Lord, when I cry aloud; be gracious to me and answer me!

8. You have said, "Seek my face." My heart says to you, "Your face, Lord, do I seek."

9. Hide not your face from me. Turn not your servant away in anger, O you who have been my help. Cast me not off; forsake me not, O God of my salvation!

10. For my father and my mother have forsaken me, but the Lord will take me in.

11. Teach me your way, O Lord, and lead me on a level path because of my enemies.

12. Give me not up to the will of my adversaries; for false witnesses have risen against me, and they breathe out violence.

13. I believe that I shall look upon the goodness of the Lord in the land of the living!

14. Wait for the Lord; be strong, and let your heart take courage; wait for the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 27 is a powerful psalm of trust and confidence in God's protection, seeking His presence and guidance in the midst of challenges. The psalmist proclaims the Lord as their light, salvation, and stronghold, expressing unwavering faith and assurance.

The psalmist acknowledges that even when evildoers and enemies come against them, they will stumble and fall. They remain fearless and confident, knowing that God is with them.

The psalmist expresses a deep longing to dwell in the presence of the Lord, to gaze upon His beauty, and to seek His wisdom in His temple. They trust that God will provide shelter and protection in times of trouble, concealing them under His tent and lifting them high upon a rock.

The psalmist anticipates victory over their enemies, rejoicing in the opportunity to offer sacrifices with shouts of joy and make melody to the Lord. They cry out to God, asking for His grace and answering their prayers. The psalmist seeks God's face, expressing a heartfelt desire for His continued help and guidance, asking not to be cast off or forsaken.

The psalmist recognizes that even if their earthly relationships fail, the Lord will take them in and be their source of comfort and support. They ask God to teach them His ways and lead them on a level path, especially in the presence of their enemies and false witnesses who seek to harm them.

In verses 13 and 14, the psalmist declares their belief in seeing the goodness of the Lord in the land of the living. They encourage waiting for the Lord, being strong, and letting their heart take courage. The psalm ends with a reaffirmation of the call to wait for the Lord.

Psalm 27 inspires confidence in God's protection and guidance, even in the face of adversity. It speaks of seeking God's presence, relying on His help, and anticipating His goodness. The psalmist's unwavering trust in the Lord serves as an example of faith and encourages others to wait patiently and take courage in God's timing and faithfulness.

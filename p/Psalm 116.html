Psalm 116

1. I love the Lord, because he has heard my voice and my pleas for mercy.

2. Because he inclined his ear to me, therefore I will call on him as long as I live.

3. The snares of death encompassed me; the pangs of Sheol laid hold on me; I suffered distress and anguish.

4. Then I called on the name of the Lord: "O Lord, I pray, deliver my soul!"

5. Gracious is the Lord, and righteous; our God is merciful.

6. The Lord preserves the simple; when I was brought low, he saved me.

7. Return, O my soul, to your rest; for the Lord has dealt bountifully with you.

8. For you have delivered my soul from death, my eyes from tears, my feet from stumbling;

9. I will walk before the Lord in the land of the living.

10. I believed, even when I spoke, "I am greatly afflicted";

11. I said in my alarm, "All mankind are liars."

12. What shall I render to the Lord for all his benefits to me?

13. I will lift up the cup of salvation and call on the name of the Lord,

14. I will pay my vows to the Lord in the presence of all his people.

15. Precious in the sight of the Lord is the death of his saints.

16. O Lord, I am your servant; I am your servant, the son of your maidservant. You have loosed my bonds.

17. I will offer to you the sacrifice of thanksgiving and call on the name of the Lord.

18. I will pay my vows to the Lord in the presence of all his people,

19. in the courts of the house of the Lord, in your midst, O Jerusalem. Praise the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 116 is a psalm of thanksgiving and praise to the Lord for His deliverance and mercy. The psalmist expresses deep gratitude for being heard by the Lord and recounts the distress and anguish they experienced, calling upon the Lord for deliverance.

The psalmist declares their love for the Lord because He has heard their voice and pleas for mercy. They acknowledge that the Lord inclined His ear to them, and as a result, they will continue to call upon Him as long as they live.

The psalmist describes the dire situation they faced, with the snares of death and the pangs of Sheol surrounding them. They experienced great distress and anguish. In their desperation, they called on the name of the Lord, praying for the deliverance of their soul.

The psalmist affirms the graciousness, righteousness, and mercy of the Lord. They acknowledge that the Lord preserves the simple and saved them when they were brought low. They encourage their soul to find rest in the Lord, recognizing the Lord's bountiful dealings with them.

The psalmist rejoices in the deliverance the Lord has granted, rescuing their soul from death, their eyes from tears, and their feet from stumbling. They declare their intention to walk before the Lord in the land of the living.

The psalmist reflects on their past struggles and moments of doubt. They confess that even in their affliction, they continued to believe. They recognized the faithfulness of the Lord in contrast to the falsehood of mankind.

The psalmist contemplates how they can repay the Lord for His abundant benefits. They resolve to lift up the cup of salvation and call upon the name of the Lord. They express their commitment to fulfill their vows to the Lord in the presence of His people.

The psalmist acknowledges the preciousness of the death of the Lord's saints in His sight. They declare themselves as the Lord's servant, rejoicing in their release from bondage. They express their intention to offer sacrifices of thanksgiving and fulfill their vows in the presence of all the people, specifically in the courts of the house of the Lord in Jerusalem.

Psalm 116 serves as a testament to the psalmist's personal experience of the Lord's deliverance and mercy. It is a heartfelt expression of thanksgiving, love, and devotion to the Lord, acknowledging His faithfulness and the psalmist's commitment to worship and serve Him.

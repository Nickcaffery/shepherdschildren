Psalm 66

1. Shout for joy to God, all the earth;

2. sing the glory of his name; give to him glorious praise!

3. Say to God, "How awesome are your deeds! So great is your power that your enemies come cringing to you.

4. All the earth worships you and sings praises to you; they sing praises to your name." Selah

5. Come and see what God has done: he is awesome in his deeds toward the children of man.

6. He turned the sea into dry land; they passed through the river on foot. There did we rejoice in him,

7. who rules by his might forever, whose eyes keep watch on the nationsâ€” let not the rebellious exalt themselves. Selah

8. Bless our God, O peoples; let the sound of his praise be heard,

9. who has kept our soul among the living and has not let our feet slip.

10. For you, O God, have tested us; you have tried us as silver is tried.

11. You brought us into the net; you laid a crushing burden on our backs;

12. you let men ride over our heads; we went through fire and through water; yet you have brought us out to a place of abundance.

13. I will come into your house with burnt offerings; I will perform my vows to you,

14. that which my lips uttered and my mouth promised when I was in trouble.

15. I will offer to you burnt offerings of fattened animals, with the smoke of the sacrifice of rams; I will make an offering of bulls and goats. Selah

16. Come and hear, all you who fear God,

17. I cried to him with my mouth, and high praise was on my tongue.

18. If I had cherished iniquity in my heart, the Lord would not have listened.

19. But truly God has listened; he has attended to the voice of my prayer.

20. Blessed be God, because he has not rejected my prayer or removed his steadfast love from me!

Commentary

Psalm 66 is a song of praise and thanksgiving to God for His mighty deeds and faithfulness. It invites all the earth to join in joyful worship and declares the awesomeness of God's power and the wonders He has performed.

The psalm begins with a call for the whole earth to shout for joy and sing praises to God's name. It acknowledges God's awesome deeds and His great power, which causes even His enemies to come cringing before Him. All the earth worships and sings praises to God.

The psalmist invites others to come and see what God has done, emphasizing His awe-inspiring deeds among humanity. They recall the crossing of the sea on dry land and passing through the river on foot, rejoicing in God's presence. The psalmist acknowledges God's eternal rule and His watchful eyes over the nations, cautioning against rebellion.

The psalmist blesses God on behalf of the people, acknowledging His preservation of their souls and protection from stumbling. They recognize that God has tested them like silver and brought them through trials, burdens, and difficult circumstances. Yet, God has led them to a place of abundance.

The psalmist expresses their intention to come into God's house with burnt offerings and fulfill their vows made during times of trouble. They offer sacrifices of burnt offerings and make offerings of fattened animals, rams, bulls, and goats.

The psalmist invites all who fear God to come and hear about God's faithfulness and the psalmist's personal experience. They proclaim their cry to God and the high praise on their tongue. The psalmist acknowledges that if they had cherished iniquity in their heart, the Lord would not have listened, but they testify that God has indeed listened and attended to their prayers.

The psalm concludes with a declaration of blessing to God, acknowledging His acceptance of prayer and His unwavering steadfast love.

Psalm 66 is a celebration of God's power, faithfulness, and answered prayers. It encourages all people to join in joyful praise and invites them to witness and reflect on God's mighty works. The psalmist gives thanks for God's deliverance and expresses confidence in His continued love and care.

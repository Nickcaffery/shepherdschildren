Psalm 29

1. Ascribe to the Lord, O heavenly beings, ascribe to the Lord glory and strength.

2. Ascribe to the Lord the glory due his name; worship the Lord in the splendor of holiness.

3. The voice of the Lord is over the waters; the God of glory thunders, the Lord, over many waters.

4. The voice of the Lord is powerful; the voice of the Lord is full of majesty.

5. The voice of the Lord breaks the cedars; the Lord breaks the cedars of Lebanon.

6. He makes Lebanon to skip like a calf, and Sirion like a young wild ox.

7. The voice of the Lord flashes forth flames of fire.

8. The voice of the Lord shakes the wilderness; the Lord shakes the wilderness of Kadesh.

9. The voice of the Lord makes the deer give birth and strips the forests bare, and in his temple all cry, "Glory!"

10. The Lord sits enthroned over the flood; the Lord sits enthroned as king forever.

11. May the Lord give strength to his people! May the Lord bless his people with peace!

Commentary

Psalm 29 is a hymn of praise that exalts the power and majesty of the Lord. It calls on heavenly beings to ascribe glory and strength to the Lord and to worship Him in His holiness. The psalm describes the voice of the Lord as a manifestation of His power, capable of breaking and shaking the earth.

The psalmist acknowledges the thunderous voice of the Lord, which echoes over the waters and displays His glory. The voice of the Lord is powerful and majestic, capable of breaking the mighty cedars of Lebanon and causing the land to tremble.

The voice of the Lord is likened to flashes of fire, shaking the wilderness and causing the deer to give birth. It reveals the power and sovereignty of God over creation. In His temple, all cry out in awe of His glory.

The psalm concludes by affirming that the Lord is enthroned over the flood and is the eternal King. The psalmist prays for the Lord to give strength to His people and bless them with peace.

Psalm 29 is a powerful declaration of the greatness and majesty of God. It emphasizes the awe-inspiring nature of His voice and the profound impact it has on creation. The psalmist invites all to worship and ascribe glory to the Lord, recognizing His power and authority over all things. The psalm concludes with a plea for God's strength and blessing upon His people.

Psalm 82

1. God has taken his place in the divine council; in the midst of the gods he holds judgment:

2. "How long will you judge unjustly and show partiality to the wicked? Selah

3. Give justice to the weak and the fatherless; maintain the right of the afflicted and the destitute.

4. Rescue the weak and the needy; deliver them from the hand of the wicked."

5. They have neither knowledge nor understanding, they walk about in darkness; all the foundations of the earth are shaken.

6. I said, "You are gods, sons of the Most High, all of you;

7. nevertheless, like men you shall die, and fall like any prince."

8. Arise, O God, judge the earth; for you shall inherit all the nations!

Commentary

Psalm 82 is a poetic expression of divine judgment. It begins by describing God presiding over the divine council, where He holds judgment among the gods. The term "gods" here likely refers to divine beings or heavenly beings rather than human rulers or judges.

The psalmist questions the actions of these divine beings, asking how long they will judge unjustly and show favoritism to the wicked. They call for justice to be given to the weak, the fatherless, the afflicted, and the destitute. They implore the divine beings to rescue and deliver the vulnerable from the hand of the wicked.

The psalmist criticizes these divine beings, highlighting their lack of knowledge and understanding. They describe them as walking in darkness, causing the foundations of the earth to be shaken. This may imply that their unjust actions have disrupted the order and well-being of society.

The psalmist then quotes God speaking to these divine beings, acknowledging them as "gods" and "sons of the Most High." However, they are warned that despite their divine status, they will die like mortals and fall like any prince or ruler.

In the final verses, the psalmist calls upon God to arise and judge the earth. They recognize God as the ultimate judge who will inherit all the nations. This concluding plea expresses the psalmist's trust in God's righteous judgment and ultimate authority.

Psalm 82 serves as a call for justice and a reminder of God's role as the ultimate judge. It critiques the unjust actions of divine beings in the divine council and calls for them to uphold justice, especially for the vulnerable and oppressed. The psalmist ultimately affirms God's supreme authority and appeals for His intervention and righteous judgment on the earth.

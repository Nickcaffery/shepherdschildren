Psalm 108

1. My heart is steadfast, O God! I will sing and make melody with all my being!

2. Awake, O harp and lyre! I will awake the dawn!

3. I will give thanks to you, O Lord, among the peoples; I will sing praises to you among the nations.

4. For your steadfast love is great above the heavens; your faithfulness reaches to the clouds.

5. Be exalted, O God, above the heavens! Let your glory be over all the earth!

6. That your beloved ones may be delivered, give salvation by your right hand and answer me!

7. God has promised in his holiness: "With exultation I will divide up Shechem and portion out the Valley of Succoth.

8. Gilead is mine; Manasseh is mine; Ephraim is my helmet; Judah is my scepter.

9. Moab is my washbasin; upon Edom I cast my shoe; over Philistia I shout in triumph."

10. Who will bring me to the fortified city? Who will lead me to Edom?

11. Have you not rejected us, O God? You do not go out, O God, with our armies.

12. Oh grant us help against the foe, for vain is the salvation of man!

13. With God we shall do valiantly; it is he who will tread down our foes.

Commentary

Psalm 108 is a psalm of praise and petition. It expresses the psalmist's steadfast devotion to God, giving thanks and singing praises. The psalmist calls upon musical instruments to accompany their worship and awakens the dawn with melodies.

The psalmist acknowledges the greatness of God's steadfast love, which extends beyond the heavens, and His faithfulness, which reaches to the clouds. They exalt God above the heavens and desire His glory to be known throughout the earth.

The psalmist appeals to God for deliverance and salvation. They ask for God's intervention and answer to their prayers. The psalmist recalls God's promises in His holiness, mentioning the division of Shechem, the portioning of the Valley of Succoth, and the possession of Gilead, Manasseh, Ephraim, Judah, Moab, Edom, and Philistia. These references symbolize God's authority and victory over various territories.

The psalmist questions who will lead them to the fortified city and Edom, implying the need for God's guidance and strength in overcoming obstacles. They express a sense of rejection and acknowledge that human efforts alone are in vain. The psalmist implores God for help against their enemies.

The psalm concludes with a declaration of faith and confidence in God's power. The psalmist proclaims that with God, they will do valiantly and that He will tread down their foes.

Psalm 108 is a heartfelt expression of praise, a plea for divine intervention, and a declaration of trust in God's deliverance. It emphasizes God's sovereignty, faithfulness, and the need to rely on Him in all circumstances.

Psalm 54

1. Save me, O God, by your name; vindicate me by your might.

2. Hear my prayer, O God; give ear to the words of my mouth.

3. For strangers have risen against me; ruthless men seek my life; they do not set God before themselves. Selah

4. Behold, God is my helper; the Lord is the upholder of my life.

5. He will return the evil to my enemies; in your faithfulness put an end to them.

6. With a freewill offering I will sacrifice to you; I will give thanks to your name, O Lord, for it is good.

7. For he has delivered me from every trouble, and my eye has looked in triumph on my enemies.

Commentary

Psalm 54 is a prayer of David, seeking God's deliverance and protection against his enemies. It expresses trust in God's name, might, and faithfulness.

David describes the situation he is facing, where strangers and ruthless men have risen against him, seeking his life. These individuals do not set God before themselves, indicating their lack of reverence for God.

Nevertheless, David affirms that God is his helper and the upholder of his life. He trusts that God will return the evil upon his enemies and put an end to their schemes, relying on God's faithfulness.

In response to God's deliverance, David declares his intention to offer a freewill offering and give thanks to the Lord's name, acknowledging its goodness. He rejoices in God's deliverance from every trouble and expresses confidence that he will see triumph over his enemies.

Psalm 54 portrays a heartfelt plea for God's intervention and protection against adversaries. It demonstrates trust in God's power and faithfulness, recognizing His ability to deliver from trouble and defeat enemies. The psalm concludes with gratitude and a declaration of praise for God's goodness and triumph over adversaries.

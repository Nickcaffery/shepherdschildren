Psalm 71

1. In you, O Lord, do I take refuge; let me never be put to shame!

2. In your righteousness deliver me and rescue me; incline your ear to me, and save me!

3. Be to me a rock of refuge, to which I may continually come; you have given the command to save me, for you are my rock and my fortress.

4. Rescue me, O my God, from the hand of the wicked, from the grasp of the unjust and cruel man.

5. For you, O Lord, are my hope, my trust, O Lord, from my youth.

6. Upon you I have leaned from before my birth; you are he who took me from my mother's womb. My praise is continually of you.

7. I have been as a portent to many, but you are my strong refuge.

8. My mouth is filled with your praise, and with your glory all the day.

9. Do not cast me off in the time of old age; forsake me not when my strength is spent.

10. For my enemies speak concerning me; those who watch for my life consult together

11. and say, "God has forsaken him; pursue and seize him, for there is none to deliver him."

12. O God, be not far from me; O my God, make haste to help me!

13. May my accusers be put to shame and consumed; with scorn and disgrace may they be covered who seek my hurt.

14. But I will hope continually and will praise you yet more and more.

15. My mouth will tell of your righteous acts, of your deeds of salvation all the day, for their number is past my knowledge.

16. With the mighty deeds of the Lord God I will come; I will remind them of your righteousness, yours alone.

17. O God, from my youth you have taught me, and I still proclaim your wondrous deeds.

18. So even to old age and gray hairs, O God, do not forsake me, until I proclaim your might to another generation, your power to all those to come.

19. Your righteousness, O God, reaches the high heavens. You who have done great things, O God, who is like you?

20. You who have made me see many troubles and calamities will revive me again; from the depths of the earth you will bring me up again.

21. You will increase my greatness and comfort me again.

22. I will also praise you with the harp for your faithfulness, O my God; I will sing praises to you with the lyre, O Holy One of Israel.

23. My lips will shout for joy, when I sing praises to you; my soul also, which you have redeemed.

24. And my tongue will talk of your righteous help all the day long, for they have been put to shame and disappointed who sought to do me hurt.

Commentary

Psalm 71 is a heartfelt prayer of an individual seeking refuge, deliverance, and continued trust in God throughout their life. It expresses praise and gratitude to God for His faithfulness and recounts the psalmist's hope and reliance on Him from their youth to old age.

The psalmist begins by declaring their trust in God and seeking refuge in Him, asking to never be put to shame. They appeal to God's righteousness, asking for deliverance, rescue, and salvation. They view God as their rock of refuge, a place of continual safety and protection.

The psalmist implores God to rescue them from the hand of the wicked and the unjust, seeking deliverance from those who seek to harm them. They express their hope and trust in the Lord, which has been present since their youth. They acknowledge God as the one who brought them forth from their mother's womb and express continuous praise to Him.

The psalmist reflects on their life, acknowledging that they have been a portent to many, but God remains their strong refuge. Their mouth is filled with praise and glory for God all day long. They ask God not to forsake them in their old age and weakness. They mention their enemies who speak against them, claiming that God has forsaken them and encouraging their pursuit.

The psalmist cries out to God, asking Him to be near and quick to help. They desire that their accusers be put to shame and consumed, covered with scorn and disgrace. Despite the challenges, the psalmist declares their unwavering hope and commitment to praise God even more. They proclaim God's righteous acts and deeds of salvation throughout the day, acknowledging that they are beyond their comprehension. They express their intent to proclaim God's righteousness and mighty deeds to future generations.

The psalmist acknowledges God's teaching and guidance since their youth and commits to proclaiming God's wondrous deeds, even in old age and gray hairs. They appeal to God not to forsake them until they have proclaimed His might and power to the next generation. The psalmist extols God's righteousness, declaring that it reaches the highest heavens and marveling at His great works.

The psalmist recognizes the troubles and calamities they have faced but expresses confidence that God will revive them and bring them up again from the depths. They believe that God will increase their greatness and comfort them once more.

The psalmist commits to praising God with musical instruments and singing praises to the Holy One of Israel. They anticipate shouting for joy and praising God with their lips and their redeemed soul. They affirm their dedication to talk about God's righteous help all day long, as those who sought to harm them have been put to shame and disappointed.

Psalm 71 is a heartfelt prayer of trust, praise, and dedication to God throughout the stages of life. It expresses the psalmist's reliance on God's faithfulness and their desire to proclaim His righteousness and mighty deeds to future generations. The psalmist's hope and gratitude shine through their words as they seek God's continued presence and deliverance.

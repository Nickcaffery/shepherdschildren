Psalm 111

1. Praise the Lord! I will give thanks to the Lord with my whole heart, in the company of the upright, in the congregation.

2. Great are the works of the Lord, studied by all who delight in them.

3. Full of splendor and majesty is his work, and his righteousness endures forever.

4. He has caused his wondrous works to be remembered; the Lord is gracious and merciful.

5. He provides food for those who fear him; he remembers his covenant forever.

6. He has shown his people the power of his works, in giving them the inheritance of the nations.

7. The works of his hands are faithful and just; all his precepts are trustworthy;

8. they are established forever and ever, to be performed with faithfulness and uprightness.

9. He sent redemption to his people; he has commanded his covenant forever. Holy and awesome is his name!

10. The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom; all those who practice it have a good understanding. His praise endures forever!

Commentary

Psalm 111 is a psalm of praise and thanksgiving to the Lord. The psalmist expresses their intention to give thanks to the Lord with their whole heart in the company of the upright and in the congregation. They acknowledge the greatness of God's works and delight in studying and contemplating them.

The psalmist describes God's works as full of splendor, majesty, and righteousness. They emphasize the enduring nature of God's righteousness and how He has caused His wondrous works to be remembered throughout generations. The psalmist proclaims that the Lord is gracious and merciful, providing food for those who fear Him and remembering His covenant forever.

The psalmist highlights how God has demonstrated His power through His works and has given His people the inheritance of the nations. They affirm the faithfulness and justice of God's works, emphasizing the trustworthiness of His precepts. The psalmist declares that God's works are established forever and to be performed with faithfulness and uprightness.

The psalmist acknowledges that God has sent redemption to His people and has commanded His covenant to endure forever. They proclaim the holiness and awe-inspiring nature of God's name. The psalmist concludes by asserting that the fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom. They affirm that those who practice the fear of the Lord have a good understanding. The psalmist acknowledges that God's praise endures forever.

Psalm 111 is a hymn of praise that magnifies the greatness of God's works, His faithfulness, and His covenant. It highlights the provision, redemption, and power of God, emphasizing the fear of the Lord as the foundation of wisdom. The psalmist's intention is to give thanks and honor to God for His wondrous deeds and enduring righteousness.

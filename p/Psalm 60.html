Psalm 60

1. O God, you have rejected us, broken our defenses; you have been angry; oh, restore us.

2. You have made the land to quake; you have torn it open; repair its breaches, for it totters.

3. You have made your people see hard things; you have given us wine to drink that made us stagger.

4. You have set up a banner for those who fear you, that they may flee to it from the bow. Selah

5. That your beloved ones may be delivered, give salvation by your right hand and answer us!

6. God has spoken in his holiness: "With exultation I will divide up Shechem and portion out the Valley of Succoth.

7. Gilead is mine; Manasseh is mine; Ephraim is my helmet; Judah is my scepter.

8. Moab is my washbasin; upon Edom I cast my shoe; over Philistia I shout in triumph."

9. Who will bring me to the fortified city? Who will lead me to Edom?

10. Have you not rejected us, O God? You do not go out, O God, with our armies.

11. Oh, grant us help against the foe, for vain is the salvation of man!

12. With God we shall do valiantly; it is he who will tread down our foes.

Commentary

Psalm 60 is attributed to David and reflects a time of distress and defeat for Israel. It portrays a plea for restoration, divine intervention, and victory over enemies.

David begins by acknowledging that God has rejected them and broken down their defenses. He appeals to God to restore them and repair the land, which is tottering due to the upheaval caused by God.

David acknowledges that God has allowed the people to experience hard things and be staggered like those who have drunk wine. The nation is in a state of turmoil and confusion.

In verse 4, David speaks of God setting up a banner for those who fear Him. This banner serves as a rallying point for God's beloved ones to seek deliverance and salvation. David implores God to answer and extend His right hand to save His people.

David declares that God has spoken in His holiness, proclaiming the division and possession of various regions. Shechem, the Valley of Succoth, Gilead, Manasseh, Ephraim, Judah, Moab, Edom, and Philistia are mentioned. These references symbolize God's authority and control over the territories.

David then expresses his desire to reach the fortified city and Edom, seeking victory over their enemies. He questions whether God has rejected them and if He no longer goes out with their armies. David appeals for God's help against their foes, acknowledging the futility of relying on human salvation.

The psalm concludes with a declaration of faith in God's power. David affirms that with God, they will do valiantly and it is He who will trample down their enemies.

Psalm 60 represents a plea for restoration, divine intervention, and victory over adversaries. It reflects the nation's acknowledgment of their current state of defeat and their reliance on God's power to bring about deliverance and triumph. The psalm encourages believers to turn to God in times of distress and trust in His strength to overcome their enemies.

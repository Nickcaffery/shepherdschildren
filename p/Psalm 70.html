Psalm 70

1. Make haste, O God, to deliver me! O Lord, make haste to help me!

2. Let them be put to shame and confusion who seek my life! Let them be turned back and brought to dishonor who delight in my hurt!

3. Let them turn back because of their shame who say, "Aha, Aha!"

4. May all who seek you rejoice and be glad in you! May those who love your salvation say evermore, "God is great!"

5. But I am poor and needy; hasten to me, O God! You are my help and my deliverer; O Lord, do not delay!

Commentary

Psalm 70 is a brief and urgent plea for God's swift deliverance and assistance. It expresses the psalmist's desire for the defeat and shame of their enemies, and it ends with a declaration of trust in God's help and deliverance.

The psalm begins with an urgent cry for God to make haste and deliver the psalmist. The psalmist implores God to quickly come to their aid.

The psalmist asks that those who seek their life be put to shame and confusion. They desire that those who delight in their harm be turned back and brought to dishonor. The psalmist prays for their enemies to experience shame and humiliation, particularly those who mockingly say, "Aha, Aha!"

In contrast to the enemies, the psalmist calls upon those who seek God to rejoice and be glad in Him. They encourage those who love God's salvation to continually declare, "God is great!" This is a proclamation of praise and exaltation of God's greatness.

The psalmist acknowledges their own state of poverty and neediness. They implore God to hasten to them, recognizing Him as their help and deliverer. The psalmist appeals to God not to delay in providing assistance.

Psalm 70 is a passionate and direct prayer for deliverance and help. It expresses the psalmist's plea for the defeat and shame of their enemies and their unwavering trust in God as their ultimate source of help and deliverance.

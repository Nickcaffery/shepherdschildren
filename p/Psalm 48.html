Psalm 48

1. Great is the Lord and greatly to be praised in the city of our God! His holy mountain,

2. beautiful in elevation, is the joy of all the earth, Mount Zion, in the far north, the city of the great King.

3. Within her citadels God has made himself known as a fortress.

4. For behold, the kings assembled; they came on together.

5. As soon as they saw it, they were astounded; they were in panic; they took to flight.

6. Trembling took hold of them there, anguish as of a woman in labor.

7. By the east wind you shattered the ships of Tarshish.

8. As we have heard, so have we seen in the city of the Lord of hosts, in the city of our God, which God will establish forever. Selah

9. We have thought on your steadfast love, O God, in the midst of your temple.

10. As your name, O God, so your praise reaches to the ends of the earth. Your right hand is filled with righteousness.

11. Let Mount Zion be glad! Let the daughters of Judah rejoice because of your judgments!

12. Walk about Zion, go around her, number her towers,

13. consider well her ramparts, go through her citadels, that you may tell the next generation

14. that this is God, our God forever and ever. He will guide us forever.

Commentary

Psalm 48 is a song of praise and celebration for the greatness and steadfast love of the Lord in the city of God, Mount Zion. The psalmist exalts God, declaring Him to be greatly praised and describing Mount Zion as the joy of the earth and the city of the great King.

The psalmist emphasizes the strength and security found in the city of God, as God has made Himself known there as a fortress. They recount a scene where kings assemble against the city, but upon witnessing its splendor and power, they are overcome with astonishment, panic, and flee in trembling. The psalmist attributes this victory to the Lord, who shatters the ships of Tarshish with the east wind.

The psalmist reflects on the steadfast love of God in the midst of His temple. They declare that God's name and praise reach to the ends of the earth, and His right hand is filled with righteousness. They call for Mount Zion and the daughters of Judah to be glad and rejoice because of God's judgments.

The psalmist encourages a thorough exploration of Mount Zion, urging people to walk about, number its towers, and consider its ramparts and citadels. They emphasize the importance of passing down this knowledge to the next generation, so they may know and declare that this is God, their God forever and ever. The psalm concludes with the affirmation that God will guide them forever.

Psalm 48 celebrates the greatness of the Lord and His steadfast love in the city of God, Mount Zion. It highlights the strength and security found in God's presence and recounts a victory over the kings who sought to attack the city. The psalmist calls for rejoicing and reflection on the splendor of Mount Zion, emphasizing the importance of passing down this knowledge to future generations. The psalm affirms God's eternal guidance and presence with His people.

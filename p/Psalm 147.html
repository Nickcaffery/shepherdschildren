Psalm 147

1. Praise the Lord! For it is good to sing praises to our God; for it is pleasant, and a song of praise is fitting.

2. The Lord builds up Jerusalem; he gathers the outcasts of Israel.

3. He heals the brokenhearted and binds up their wounds.

4. He determines the number of the stars; he gives to all of them their names.

5. Great is our Lord, and abundant in power; his understanding is beyond measure.

6. The Lord lifts up the humble; he casts the wicked to the ground.

7. Sing to the Lord with thanksgiving; make melody to our God on the lyre!

8. He covers the heavens with clouds; he prepares rain for the earth; he makes grass grow on the hills.

9. He gives to the beasts their food, and to the young ravens that cry.

10. His delight is not in the strength of the horse, nor his pleasure in the legs of a man,

11. but the Lord takes pleasure in those who fear him, in those who hope in his steadfast love.

12. Praise the Lord, O Jerusalem! Praise your God, O Zion!

13. For he strengthens the bars of your gates; he blesses your children within you.

14. He makes peace in your borders; he fills you with the finest of the wheat.

15. He sends out his command to the earth; his word runs swiftly.

16. He gives snow like wool; he scatters frost like ashes.

17. He hurls down his crystals of ice like crumbs; who can stand before his cold?

18. He sends out his word, and melts them; he makes his wind blow and the waters flow.

19. He declares his word to Jacob, his statutes and rules to Israel.

20. He has not dealt thus with any other nation; they do not know his rules. Praise the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 147 is a psalm of praise and thanksgiving to the Lord. The psalmist begins by exhorting the praise of the Lord, acknowledging that it is good and fitting to sing praises to Him. They emphasize the Lord's role in building up Jerusalem and gathering the outcasts of Israel. The Lord is described as the healer of the brokenhearted, binding up their wounds. The psalmist marvels at God's sovereignty over the stars, numbering them and giving them names. They proclaim the greatness of the Lord, His abundant power, and His limitless understanding. The Lord lifts up the humble but casts the wicked down to the ground.

The psalmist calls for singing and making melody to the Lord with thanksgiving, using musical instruments such as the lyre. They attribute to the Lord the ability to cover the heavens with clouds and provide rain for the earth, causing the grass to grow on the hills. The Lord ensures that the beasts and even the young ravens are fed. The psalmist contrasts the Lord's pleasure, not in physical strength or human abilities, but in those who fear Him and hope in His steadfast love.

The psalmist praises the Lord and calls upon Jerusalem and Zion to do the same. They acknowledge the Lord's role in strengthening the city's gates and blessing the children within it. The Lord brings peace to the borders and provides an abundance of wheat. The psalmist describes God's control over nature, sending out His command to the earth, causing snow, frost, and ice to form and melt. The Lord declares His word, statutes, and rules to Jacob and Israel, making known His ways to them. The psalmist acknowledges that the Lord's dealings are unique to Israel, as they are the recipients of His laws and ordinances.

The psalm concludes with a final call to praise the Lord, emphasizing that His rules are unknown to other nations. The psalmist encourages all to join in praising the Lord.

Psalm 147 is a psalm that exalts the Lord for His role as the builder of Jerusalem, the healer of the brokenhearted, and the sovereign ruler over creation. It emphasizes God's delight in those who fear Him and hope in His steadfast love. The psalmist calls for praise and thanksgiving to the Lord and marvels at His provision for the earth and His relationship with Israel. The psalm concludes with a final exhortation to praise the Lord, acknowledging His unique relationship with His people.

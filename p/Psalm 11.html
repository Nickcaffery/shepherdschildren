Psalm 11

1. In the Lord I take refuge; how can you say to my soul, "Flee like a bird to your mountain,

2. for behold, the wicked bend the bow; they have fitted their arrow to the string

3. if the foundations are destroyed, what can the righteous do?"

4. The Lord is in his holy temple; the Lord's throne is in heaven; his eyes see, his eyelids test the children of man.

5. The Lord tests the righteous, but his soul hates the wicked and the one who loves violence.

6. Let him rain coals on the wicked; fire and sulfur and a scorching wind shall be the portion of their cup.

7. For the Lord is righteous; he loves righteous deeds; the upright shall behold his face.

Commentary

Psalm 11 is a psalm of trust in God's righteousness and justice, attributed to King David. It addresses the challenges and doubts faced by the righteous in the face of wickedness and the assurance of God's presence and judgment.

The psalm begins with a declaration of taking refuge in the Lord. The psalmist questions those who urge him to flee like a bird to the mountains, suggesting that he should seek safety elsewhere. Instead, the psalmist affirms his trust in God as his refuge.

The psalmist describes the wicked who are ready to attack and harm the righteous. The imagery of bending bows and fitting arrows to the string portrays their readiness for violence.

In verse 3, the psalmist presents a rhetorical question: "If the foundations are destroyed, what can the righteous do?" This conveys a sense of hopelessness or doubt regarding the state of the world and the ability of the righteous to stand against the wicked.

However, the psalmist quickly reminds himself and others of the sovereignty and righteousness of the Lord. God is depicted as being in His holy temple and seated on His heavenly throne. His watchful eyes see and test the children of humanity.

The psalmist emphasizes that the Lord tests the righteous, examining their hearts and actions, while expressing God's hatred toward the wicked and those who love violence. The psalmist calls upon God to rain coals, fire, sulfur, and a scorching wind upon the wicked as their just retribution.

Verse 7 affirms the righteousness of the Lord. He loves righteous deeds, and the upright will have the privilege of beholding His face. This serves as a reminder of the ultimate reward and communion with God that awaits those who remain faithful and upright.

Psalm 11 encourages trust in God's righteous judgment and justice, even in the face of wickedness and doubt. It highlights the steadfastness of the Lord and His love for the upright. The psalmist finds solace in God's presence and looks forward to the righteous deeds and communion with Him.

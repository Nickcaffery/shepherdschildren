Psalm 12

1. Save, O Lord, for the godly one is gone; for the faithful have vanished from among the children of man.

2. Everyone utters lies to his neighbor; with flattering lips and a double heart they speak.

3. May the Lord cut off all flattering lips, the tongue that makes great boasts,

4. those who say, "With our tongue we will prevail, our lips are with us; who is master over us?"

5. "Because the poor are plundered, because the needy groan, I will now arise," says the Lord; "I will place him in the safety for which he longs."

6. The words of the Lord are pure words, like silver refined in a furnace on the ground, purified seven times.

7. You, O Lord, will keep them; you will guard us from this generation forever.

8. On every side the wicked prowl, as vileness is exalted among the children of man.

Commentary

Psalm 12 is a psalm of lament and trust in God's faithfulness in the midst of a corrupt and deceitful world. It addresses the prevalence of lies, flattery, and oppression, calling upon the Lord for deliverance and affirming His promise to protect the righteous.

The psalm begins with a cry for salvation, as the godly and faithful people seem to have disappeared or diminished among the children of humanity. The psalmist laments the abundance of lies and deceitful speech, with people speaking with flattering lips and double hearts.

The psalmist petitions the Lord to cut off those who use flattering lips and make great boasts. They challenge the arrogance of those who believe their speech alone will lead them to prevail, asserting their independence from any higher authority.

In verses 5 and 6, the psalm shifts to the response of the Lord. The Lord declares that He will arise and take action on behalf of the poor and the needy who are plundered and groaning. The psalmist expresses confidence in God's promise to place the oppressed in the safety they long for.

The psalmist then affirms the purity and reliability of the words of the Lord. The words of God are likened to silver refined in a furnace, purified seven times. They are seen as trustworthy and true.

The psalmist expresses trust in God's protection, acknowledging that the Lord will keep the words of promise and guard His people from the present generation and its wickedness.

The psalm concludes by noting the pervasive presence of the wicked, as vileness and corruption seem to be exalted among humanity. This underscores the need for God's intervention and deliverance.

Psalm 12 highlights the psalmist's lament over a deceitful and oppressive society. It calls upon God for deliverance and affirms His faithfulness and protection for the righteous. It acknowledges the corruption and wickedness present but places trust in God's purity and promise to guard His people.

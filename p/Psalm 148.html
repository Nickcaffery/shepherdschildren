Psalm 148

1. Praise the Lord! Praise the Lord from the heavens; praise him in the heights!

2. Praise him, all his angels; praise him, all his hosts!

3. Praise him, sun and moon, praise him, all you shining stars!

4. Praise him, you highest heavens, and you waters above the heavens!

5. Let them praise the name of the Lord! For he commanded and they were created.

6. And he established them forever and ever; he gave a decree, and it shall not pass away.

7. Praise the Lord from the earth, you great sea creatures and all deeps,

8. fire and hail, snow and mist, stormy wind fulfilling his word!

9. Mountains and all hills, fruit trees and all cedars!

10. Beasts and all livestock, creeping things and flying birds!

11. Kings of the earth and all peoples, princes and all rulers of the earth!

12. Young men and maidens together, old men and children!

13. Let them praise the name of the Lord, for his name alone is exalted; his majesty is above earth and heaven.

14. He has raised up a horn for his people, praise for all his saints, for the people of Israel who are near to him. Praise the Lord!

Commentary

Psalm 148 is a psalm of universal praise, calling upon all creation to exalt the Lord. The psalmist begins by urging praise to the Lord from the heavens and in the heights. They call upon the angels and heavenly hosts to join in praise.

The psalmist continues by summoning the sun, moon, and shining stars to praise the Lord. They call upon the highest heavens and the waters above the heavens to offer praise. The psalmist emphasizes that all of creation should praise the name of the Lord because He commanded their creation and established them forever by His decree.

The psalmist then calls for praise from the earth, addressing the great sea creatures, the depths, fire, hail, snow, mist, and stormy wind. They urge the mountains, hills, fruit trees, cedars, beasts, livestock, creeping things, and flying birds to join in praising the Lord.

The psalmist broadens the call for praise to include the kings, peoples, princes, and rulers of the earth. They highlight the unity in praise, with young men, maidens, old men, and children coming together in worship.

The psalmist emphasizes that the name of the Lord alone is exalted, and His majesty surpasses that of both earth and heaven. They recognize that the Lord has raised up a horn, symbolizing strength and honor, for His people. The psalmist expresses praise for all the saints and specifically acknowledges the people of Israel, who are near to the Lord.

The psalm concludes with a final exhortation to praise the Lord.

Psalm 148 is a psalm that calls upon all of creation, both heavenly and earthly, to praise the Lord. It emphasizes the vastness of God's creative power and the unity of all creatures in offering praise to Him. The psalmist highlights the exalted nature of the Lord's name and His special relationship with His people. The psalm concludes with a call to universal praise, acknowledging the greatness of the Lord.

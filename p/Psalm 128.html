Psalm 128

1. Blessed is everyone who fears the Lord, who walks in his ways!

2. You shall eat the fruit of the labor of your hands; you shall be blessed, and it shall be well with you.

3. Your wife will be like a fruitful vine within your house; your children will be like olive shoots around your table.

4. Behold, thus shall the man be blessed who fears the Lord.

5. The Lord bless you from Zion! May you see the prosperity of Jerusalem all the days of your life!

6. May you see your children's children! Peace be upon Israel!

Commentary

Psalm 128 is a psalm that celebrates the blessings and prosperity that come from fearing and walking in the ways of the Lord. It highlights the joy and fulfillment that individuals and their families experience when they live in accordance with God's commands.

The psalm begins by pronouncing blessings upon everyone who fears the Lord and walks in His ways. It signifies the happiness and well-being that come from living a life that is reverent and obedient to God.

The psalmist describes the fruit of one's labor as a source of sustenance and blessing. Those who fear the Lord can expect to enjoy the fruits of their labor and experience overall prosperity and well-being.

The psalmist uses the imagery of a fruitful vine and olive shoots to depict the flourishing nature of a person's household. The wife is compared to a fruitful vine, symbolizing her productivity and abundance within the home. The children are likened to olive shoots, signifying their growth and potential. This imagery emphasizes the joy and blessing of a thriving family life.

The psalmist affirms that the one who fears the Lord will be blessed in this way. The blessings of a fruitful household and a prosperous life are seen as outcomes of reverencing and following the Lord.

The psalm concludes with a blessing and a prayer. The psalmist prays for the Lord's blessing from Zion and for the continued prosperity and well-being of Jerusalem. They extend the prayer for future generations, expressing the desire to see their children's children. The psalm ends with a plea for peace upon Israel, signifying the importance of harmony and tranquility within the community.

Psalm 128 emphasizes the blessings and fulfillment that come from fearing and walking in the ways of the Lord. It highlights the joys of a flourishing family life and the overall prosperity that stems from a life of reverence and obedience. The psalm concludes with a prayer for continued blessings, prosperity, and peace upon the community of Israel.

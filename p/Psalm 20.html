Psalm 20

1. May the Lord answer you in the day of trouble! May the name of the God of Jacob protect you!

2. May he send you help from the sanctuary and give you support from Zion!

3. May he remember all your offerings and regard with favor your burnt sacrifices! Selah

4. May he grant you your heart's desire and fulfill all your plans!

5. May we shout for joy over your salvation, and in the name of our God set up our banners! May the Lord fulfill all your petitions!

6. Now I know that the Lord saves his anointed; he will answer him from his holy heaven with the saving might of his right hand.

7. Some trust in chariots and some in horses, but we trust in the name of the Lord our God.

8. They collapse and fall, but we rise and stand upright.

9. O Lord, save the king! May he answer us when we call.

Commentary

Psalm 20 is a prayer for the success and protection of the king and the people of Israel. It expresses trust in God's intervention and deliverance, highlighting the contrast between relying on earthly strength versus trusting in the Lord.

The psalm begins with a prayer for the Lord to answer in times of trouble and to provide protection. The psalmist calls upon the God of Jacob, the God of the Israelites, to send help and support from His sanctuary in Zion. They also mention the offerings and sacrifices made to God, asking for favor and remembrance of these acts of worship.

The psalmist then prays for God to grant the heart's desire of the king and to fulfill all their plans. They anticipate rejoicing over the king's salvation and raise banners in the name of their God. The psalmist asks for the Lord to answer all the petitions and prayers of the king and the people.

Verse 6 affirms the psalmist's confidence that the Lord saves His anointed one, likely referring to the king. The psalmist expects God to answer the anointed one from His holy heaven with His mighty right hand.

In verse 7, a contrast is drawn between those who trust in chariots and horses, symbols of human strength and power, and those who trust in the name of the Lord their God. The psalmist declares that while others may collapse and fall, those who trust in the Lord will rise and stand upright.

The psalm concludes with a plea for the Lord to save the king and to answer the people's call for help.

Psalm 20 expresses a prayer for the success, protection, and deliverance of the king and the people of Israel. It emphasizes trust in God's intervention and contrasts earthly strength with reliance on the name of the Lord. The psalmist acknowledges that true victory and security come from God alone and calls upon Him to save and answer their prayers.

Psalm 25

1. To you, O Lord, I lift up my soul.

2. O my God, in you I trust; let me not be put to shame; let not my enemies exult over me.

3. Indeed, none who wait for you shall be put to shame; they shall be ashamed who are wantonly treacherous.

4. Make me to know your ways, O Lord; teach me your paths.

5. Lead me in your truth and teach me, for you are the God of my salvation; for you I wait all the day long.

6. Remember your mercy, O Lord, and your steadfast love, for they have been from of old.

7. Remember not the sins of my youth or my transgressions; according to your steadfast love remember me, for the sake of your goodness, O Lord!

8. Good and upright is the Lord; therefore he instructs sinners in the way.

9. He leads the humble in what is right, and teaches the humble his way.

10. All the paths of the Lord are steadfast love and faithfulness, for those who keep his covenant and his testimonies.

11. For your name's sake, O Lord, pardon my guilt, for it is great.

12. Who is the man who fears the Lord? Him will he instruct in the way that he should choose.

13. His soul shall abide in well-being, and his offspring shall inherit the land.

14. The friendship of the Lord is for those who fear him, and he makes known to them his covenant.

15. My eyes are ever toward the Lord, for he will pluck my feet out of the net.

16. Turn to me and be gracious to me, for I am lonely and afflicted.

17. The troubles of my heart are enlarged; bring me out of my distresses.

18. Consider my affliction and my trouble, and forgive all my sins.

19. Consider how many are my foes, and with what violent hatred they hate me.

20. Oh, guard my soul, and deliver me! Let me not be put to shame, for I take refuge in you.

21. May integrity and uprightness preserve me, for I wait for you.

22. Redeem Israel, O God, out of all his troubles.

Commentary

Psalm 25 is a heartfelt prayer for guidance, forgiveness, and protection. It expresses trust in God's character and seeks His instruction and mercy. The psalmist acknowledges their sins, pleads for God's forgiveness, and places their hope in His steadfast love and faithfulness.

The psalm begins with the psalmist lifting their soul to the Lord, expressing trust and seeking deliverance from their enemies. They declare their confidence that those who wait for God will not be put to shame, while the treacherous will experience shame.

The psalmist then asks God to make them know His ways and teach them His paths. They desire to be led in truth and to receive instruction from the God of their salvation. Their waiting is focused on God throughout the day.

In verses 6 and 7, the psalmist appeals to God's mercy and steadfast love, asking Him to remember them and not hold their past sins against them. They seek forgiveness and restoration based on God's goodness.

The psalmist recognizes the Lord as good and upright, instructing sinners and leading the humble in the right way. They affirm that all of the Lord's paths are characterized by steadfast love and faithfulness for those who keep His covenant and testimonies.

For the sake of God's name, the psalmist asks for pardon and guidance. They acknowledge that those who fear the Lord will receive instruction and experience well-being. The psalmist places their trust in the friendship of the Lord and expresses their dependence on Him for deliverance from traps and distress.

In their loneliness and affliction, the psalmist pleads for God's graciousness and deliverance. They ask for the consideration of their troubles, forgiveness of their sins, and protection from their foes. The psalmist desires integrity and uprightness to preserve them as they wait for God.

The psalm concludes with a plea for God to redeem Israel from all their troubles.

Psalm 25 is a heartfelt prayer that reflects a deep sense of reliance on God's mercy, guidance, and forgiveness. It emphasizes the trustworthiness of God's ways and His steadfast love and faithfulness. The psalmist acknowledges their own shortcomings and places their hope in God's character and promises.

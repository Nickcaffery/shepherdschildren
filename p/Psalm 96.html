Psalm 96

1. Oh sing to the Lord a new song; sing to the Lord, all the earth!

2. Sing to the Lord, bless his name; tell of his salvation from day to day.

3. Declare his glory among the nations, his marvelous works among all the peoples!

4. For great is the Lord, and greatly to be praised; he is to be feared above all gods.

5. For all the gods of the peoples are worthless idols, but the Lord made the heavens.

6. Splendor and majesty are before him; strength and beauty are in his sanctuary.

7. Ascribe to the Lord, O families of the peoples, ascribe to the Lord glory and strength!

8. Ascribe to the Lord the glory due his name; bring an offering, and come into his courts!

9. Worship the Lord in the splendor of holiness; tremble before him, all the earth!

10. Say among the nations, "The Lord reigns! Yes, the world is established; it shall never be moved; he will judge the peoples with equity."

11. Let the heavens be glad, and let the earth rejoice; let the sea roar, and all that fills it;

12. let the field exult, and everything in it! Then shall all the trees of the forest sing for joy

13. before the Lord, for he comes, for he comes to judge the earth. He will judge the world in righteousness, and the peoples in his faithfulness.

Commentary

Psalm 96 is a psalm that calls for praise and worship of the Lord, emphasizing His greatness, glory, and the need to declare His salvation among the nations.

The psalmist invites the entire earth to sing a new song to the Lord and bless His name. They encourage the proclamation of His salvation on a daily basis. The psalmist urges the declaration of God's glory and marvelous works among all peoples and nations. They affirm that the Lord is great and deserving of praise, surpassing all other gods. The psalmist emphasizes the insignificance of idols, declaring that the Lord alone created the heavens.

The psalmist recognizes the splendor, majesty, strength, and beauty that surround the Lord in His sanctuary. They call upon the families of all nations to ascribe glory and strength to the Lord. The psalmist encourages the offering of worship and entrance into His courts. They exhort the trembling and awe-inspired reverence before the Lord.

The psalmist further declares the reign of the Lord among the nations, asserting the establishment and immovability of the world. They proclaim that He will judge the peoples with equity.

The psalmist calls for rejoicing and gladness in the heavens and the earth. They invite the sea and all its creatures, the field and everything within it, and the trees of the forest to exult and sing for joy before the Lord. The psalmist declares that the Lord is coming to judge the earth with righteousness and faithfulness. His judgment will extend to the entire world and its people.

Psalm 96 emphasizes the praise and worship of the Lord, highlighting His greatness, glory, and the need to proclaim His salvation among all nations. It underscores the distinction between the true God and idols, emphasizing the Lord's role as the Creator of the heavens. The psalm calls for reverence and awe before the Lord and invites all creation to rejoice and sing in anticipation of His righteous judgment.

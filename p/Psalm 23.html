Psalm 23

1. The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want.

2. He makes me lie down in green pastures. He leads me beside still waters.

3. He restores my soul. He leads me in paths of righteousness for his name's sake.

4. Even though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil, for you are with me; your rod and your staff, they comfort me.

5. You prepare a table before me in the presence of my enemies; you anoint my head with oil; my cup overflows.

6. Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life, and I shall dwell in the house of the Lord forever.

Commentary

Psalm 23 is one of the most well-known and beloved psalms, often referred to as the Shepherd's Psalm. It portrays God as a caring and protective shepherd, guiding and providing for His people. The psalmist expresses trust, comfort, and confidence in God's presence and provision.

The psalm begins with a declaration of trust: "The Lord is my shepherd; I shall not want." The psalmist acknowledges God as their shepherd, who watches over and tends to their needs. They affirm that in the care of the Lord, they lack nothing.

The psalmist then describes the shepherd's actions. God makes them lie down in green pastures and leads them beside still waters, providing nourishment and rest. The shepherd restores their soul and guides them in paths of righteousness for His name's sake, ensuring their well-being and guiding them in the right way.

Even in the face of difficult times, symbolized by the valley of the shadow of death, the psalmist expresses unwavering trust in God's protection. They declare that they will fear no evil because God is with them. The rod and staff, symbols of the shepherd's guidance and discipline, bring comfort and assurance.

In verse 5, the imagery shifts to that of a gracious host. God prepares a table before the psalmist in the presence of enemies, indicating His provision and protection even in the midst of opposition. The psalmist experiences anointing with oil, a symbol of honor and blessing. Their cup overflows, signifying abundant provision and joy.

The psalm concludes with a declaration of God's goodness and mercy. The psalmist affirms that these attributes will follow them all the days of their life. They express the hope of dwelling in the house of the Lord forever, indicating a longing for a deep and eternal relationship with God.

Psalm 23 is a comforting and reassuring psalm that portrays God as a caring and protective shepherd. It speaks of trust, provision, guidance, and comfort in the midst of life's challenges. The psalmist's words resonate with those who seek solace and find assurance in their relationship with God.

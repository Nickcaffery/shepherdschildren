Psalm 143

1. Hear my prayer, O Lord; give ear to my pleas for mercy! In your faithfulness answer me, in your righteousness!

2. Enter not into judgment with your servant, for no one living is righteous before you.

3. For the enemy has pursued my soul; he has crushed my life to the ground;

4. Therefore my spirit faints within me; my heart within me is appalled.

5. I remember the days of old; I meditate on all that you have done; I ponder the work of your hands.

6. I stretch out my hands to you; my soul thirsts for you like a parched land.

7. Answer me quickly, O Lord! My spirit fails! Hide not your face from me, lest I be like those who go down to the pit.

8. Let me hear in the morning of your steadfast love, for in you I trust. Make me know the way I should go, for to you I lift up my soul.

9. Deliver me from my enemies, O Lord! I have fled to you for refuge.

10. Teach me to do your will, for you are my God! Let your good Spirit lead me on level ground!

11. For your name's sake, O Lord, preserve my life! In your righteousness bring my soul out of trouble!

12. And in your steadfast love you will cut off my enemies, and you will destroy all the adversaries of my soul, for I am your servant.

Commentary

Psalm 143 is a prayer of the psalmist seeking the Lord's mercy, deliverance, and guidance. The psalmist pleads for God to hear their prayer, acknowledging His faithfulness and righteousness.

The psalmist humbly asks God not to enter into judgment with them because no one is righteous before Him. They describe the enemy's pursuit and the crushing of their life, causing their spirit to faint and their heart to be appalled.

In the midst of their distress, the psalmist remembers the days of old and meditates on all that God has done, pondering His works. They stretch out their hands in a posture of surrender and express their longing for God, comparing their thirst for Him to a parched land.

The psalmist urgently calls for a quick answer from the Lord, as their spirit is failing. They plead for God not to hide His face from them, fearing the fate of those who go down to the pit. The psalmist desires to hear of God's steadfast love in the morning and expresses their trust in Him. They seek to know the way they should go and lift up their soul to God.

The psalmist seeks deliverance from their enemies, acknowledging God as their refuge. They ask to be taught to do God's will, acknowledging Him as their God. They pray for the leading of God's good Spirit on level ground.

For the sake of His name, the psalmist implores the Lord to preserve their life and bring their soul out of trouble in His righteousness. They trust that God's steadfast love will bring about the destruction of their enemies and the adversaries of their soul, affirming their identity as God's servant.

Psalm 143 reflects the psalmist's plea for God's mercy, deliverance, and guidance in the midst of distress. The psalmist acknowledges God's faithfulness and righteousness and expresses their trust and longing for Him. They seek deliverance from enemies, instruction in God's will, and the leading of His Spirit. The psalmist places their hope in God's steadfast love and identifies themselves as His servant.

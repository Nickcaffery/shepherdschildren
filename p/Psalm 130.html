Psalm 130

1. Out of the depths I cry to you, O Lord!

2. O Lord, hear my voice! Let your ears be attentive to the voice of my pleas for mercy!

3. If you, O Lord, should mark iniquities, O Lord, who could stand?

4. But with you there is forgiveness, that you may be feared.

5. I wait for the Lord, my soul waits, and in his word I hope;

6. my soul waits for the Lord more than watchmen for the morning, more than watchmen for the morning.

7. O Israel, hope in the Lord! For with the Lord there is steadfast love, and with him is plentiful redemption.

8. And he will redeem Israel from all his iniquities.

Commentary

Psalm 130 is a psalm of deep personal lament and hope in the Lord's forgiveness and redemption. The psalmist cries out to the Lord from the depths, expressing the depth of their distress and need for mercy.

The psalmist pleads with the Lord to hear their voice and be attentive to their pleas for mercy. They acknowledge their own unworthiness, recognizing that if the Lord were to mark iniquities, no one could stand before Him. The psalmist highlights the profound need for forgiveness and acknowledges that it is only through the Lord's mercy and grace that they can be saved.

The psalmist expresses a posture of waiting and hoping in the Lord. They wait for the Lord with their whole being, placing their trust in His word and His promises. The psalmist compares their waiting to that of watchmen who eagerly anticipate the morning, emphasizing the intensity and perseverance of their hope in the Lord.

The psalmist addresses Israel, calling them to share in this hope in the Lord. They encourage Israel to put their trust in the steadfast love of the Lord, emphasizing His abundant redemption. The psalmist affirms the Lord's ability to redeem Israel from all their iniquities, underscoring the transformative power of God's love and grace.

Psalm 130 expresses the depths of human need and the longing for God's mercy and forgiveness. It highlights the importance of waiting, hoping, and trusting in the Lord's faithfulness and promises. The psalmist encourages the community of Israel to place their hope in the Lord, reminding them of His steadfast love and redemption. Ultimately, the psalm points to the assurance of God's redemptive work and His ability to deliver His people from their sins.

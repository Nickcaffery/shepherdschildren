Psalm 37

1. Fret not yourself because of evildoers; be not envious of wrongdoers!

2. For they will soon fade like the grass and wither like the green herb.

3. Trust in the Lord, and do good; dwell in the land and befriend faithfulness.

4. Delight yourself in the Lord, and he will give you the desires of your heart.

5. Commit your way to the Lord; trust in him, and he will act.

6. He will bring forth your righteousness as the light, and your justice as the noonday.

7. Be still before the Lord and wait patiently for him; fret not yourself over the one who prospers in his way, over the man who carries out evil devices!

8. Refrain from anger, and forsake wrath! Fret not yourself; it tends only to evil.

9. For the evildoers shall be cut off, but those who wait for the Lord shall inherit the land.

10. In just a little while, the wicked will be no more; though you look carefully at his place, he will not be there.

11. But the meek shall inherit the land and delight themselves in abundant peace.

12. The wicked plots against the righteous and gnashes his teeth at him,

13. but the Lord laughs at the wicked, for he sees that his day is coming.

14. The wicked draw the sword and bend their bows to bring down the poor and needy, to slay those whose way is upright;

15. their sword shall enter their own heart, and their bows shall be broken.

16. Better is the little that the righteous has than the abundance of many wicked.

17. For the arms of the wicked shall be broken, but the Lord upholds the righteous.

18. The Lord knows the days of the blameless, and their heritage will remain forever;

19. they are not put to shame in evil times; in the days of famine they have abundance.

20. But the wicked will perish; the enemies of the Lord are like the glory of the pastures; they vanishâ€”like smoke they vanish away.

21. The wicked borrows but does not pay back, but the righteous is generous and gives;

22. for those blessed by the Lord shall inherit the land, but those cursed by him shall be cut off.

23. The steps of a man are established by the Lord, when he delights in his way;

24. though he fall, he shall not be cast headlong, for the Lord upholds his hand.

25. I have been young, and now am old, yet I have not seen the righteous forsaken or his children begging for bread.

26. He is ever lending generously, and his children become a blessing.

27. Turn away from evil and do good; so shall you dwell forever.

28. For the Lord loves justice; he will not forsake his saints. They are preserved forever, but the children of the wicked shall be cut off.

29. The righteous shall inherit the land and dwell upon it forever.

30. The mouth of the righteous utters wisdom, and his tongue speaks justice.

31. The law of his God is in his heart; his steps do not slip.

32. The wicked watches for the righteous and seeks to put him to death.

33. The Lord will not abandon him to his power or let him be condemned when he is brought to trial.

34. Wait for the Lord and keep his way, and he will exalt you to inherit the land; you will look on when the wicked are cut off.

35. I have seen a wicked, ruthless man, spreading himself like a green laurel tree.

36. But he passed away, and behold, he was no more; though I sought him, he could not be found.

37. Mark the blameless and behold the upright, for there is a future for the man of peace.

38. But transgressors shall be altogether destroyed; the future of the wicked shall be cut off.

39. The salvation of the righteous is from the Lord; he is their stronghold in the time of trouble.

40. The Lord helps them and delivers them; he delivers them from the wicked and saves them, because they take refuge in him.

Commentary

Psalm 37 provides guidance and encouragement to trust in the Lord, refrain from worrying about evildoers, and remain faithful in doing good. It contrasts the temporary nature of the wicked with the security and blessings of the righteous. The psalm emphasizes the importance of patience, righteousness, and reliance on the Lord's provision and protection.

The psalm begins by urging against fretting or being envious of evildoers, assuring that they will fade and wither away. Instead, the psalmist advises trust in the Lord and doing good, dwelling in the land and being faithful. Delighting in the Lord leads to the fulfillment of the desires of the heart.

The psalmist encourages committing one's way to the Lord and trusting in Him, promising that He will act on behalf of the righteous, bringing forth their righteousness and justice. The psalmist calls for stillness and patient waiting for the Lord, discouraging worry over those who prosper through evil schemes. The psalmist urges refraining from anger and forsaking wrath, as they lead to evil. The evildoers will be cut off, but those who wait for the Lord will inherit the land.

The psalm contrasts the destiny of the wicked with that of the meek, affirming that the meek will inherit the land and experience abundant peace. The wicked plot against the righteous, but the Lord laughs at them, knowing their day of judgment is coming. The wicked seek to harm the poor and upright, but their own weapons will turn against them. The arms of the wicked will be broken, while the Lord upholds the righteous.

The psalm reassures that the blameless are known by the Lord, and their heritage remains forever. They will not be put to shame in times of evil or famine. In contrast, the wicked will perish and vanish away. The righteous lend generously and are blessed, while the wicked borrow but do not repay.

The psalmist acknowledges that the steps of a person are established by the Lord when they delight in His way. Though they may stumble, they will not be cast down because the Lord upholds them. The psalmist reflects on their own experience, testifying that they have never seen the righteous forsaken or their children begging for bread. The righteous are generous and their children become a blessing.

The psalmist advises turning away from evil and doing good, assuring that those who do so will dwell forever. The Lord loves justice and preserves His saints. The righteous will inherit the land and dwell upon it forever. The mouth of the righteous speaks wisdom and justice, and their steps do not slip.

The psalm acknowledges that the wicked watch for the righteous and seek to harm them, but the Lord will not abandon the righteous or let them be condemned. The psalmist urges waiting for the Lord and following His way, promising that He will exalt and allow the righteous to witness the downfall of the wicked.

The psalmist reflects on observing a wicked and ruthless man who seemed powerful and prosperous, but ultimately passed away and could not be found. The psalmist encourages marking the blameless and upright, for they have a future of peace. Transgressorsshall be destroyed, and the future of the wicked shall be cut off.

The psalmist affirms that the salvation of the righteous comes from the Lord, who serves as their stronghold in times of trouble. The Lord helps and delivers them, rescuing them from the wicked, because they take refuge in Him.

Psalm 37 offers guidance for living a righteous and faithful life. It encourages trust in the Lord, patience, and refraining from worry or envy. It highlights the temporary nature of evildoers and the security and blessings that come from following the Lord's ways. The psalm assures that the Lord upholds the righteous, who will inherit the land and experience peace. It emphasizes the importance of turning away from evil, doing good, and taking refuge in the Lord, who delivers His faithful servants.

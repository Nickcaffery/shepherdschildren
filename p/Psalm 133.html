Psalm 133

1. Behold, how good and pleasant it is when brothers dwell in unity!

2. It is like the precious oil on the head, running down on the beard, on the beard of Aaron, running down on the collar of his robes!

3. It is like the dew of Hermon, which falls on the mountains of Zion! For there the Lord has commanded the blessing, life forevermore.

Commentary

Psalm 133 is a short psalm that celebrates the beauty and importance of unity among brothers. It likens the experience of dwelling in unity to precious anointing oil and refreshing dew, emphasizing the blessings that come from harmony and togetherness.

The psalmist declares that it is good and pleasant when brothers live together in unity. Unity among family members, specifically brothers, is seen as something desirable and beneficial. The psalmist recognizes the value of peaceful relationships and cooperation within the community.

To illustrate the goodness of unity, the psalmist uses two vivid metaphors. Firstly, they compare it to the precious anointing oil poured on the head of Aaron, the high priest of Israel. The oil symbolizes consecration, blessing, and the presence of the Lord. Just as the anointing oil flows down from Aaron's head to his beard and robe, the psalmist envisions unity spreading and encompassing the entire community.

Secondly, the psalmist compares unity to the dew of Hermon, a mountain in the region of Mount Zion. The dew is refreshing and brings nourishment to the land, contributing to its fertility. The image portrays the life-giving and rejuvenating qualities of unity, much like the dew that brings vitality and growth to the mountains of Zion.

The psalm concludes by acknowledging that it is in the place of unity, where the Lord has commanded His blessing. The blessings of life and divine favor are associated with unity among brothers. The psalmist affirms that when people dwell together in harmony, they experience the fullness of God's blessings.

Psalm 133 highlights the importance and beauty of unity among brothers. It emphasizes the goodness and pleasantness of living in harmony and cooperation. The psalmist uses powerful metaphors of anointing oil and dew to convey the transformative and life-giving qualities of unity. The psalm concludes with the assurance that God blesses those who dwell in unity, granting them abundant life and favor.

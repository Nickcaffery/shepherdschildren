Psalm 140

1. Deliver me, O Lord, from evil men; preserve me from violent men,

2. who plan evil things in their heart and stir up wars continually.

3. They make their tongue sharp as a serpent's, and under their lips is the venom of asps.

4. Guard me, O Lord, from the hands of the wicked; preserve me from violent men, who have planned to trip up my feet.

5. The arrogant have hidden a trap for me, and with cords they have spread a net; beside the way they have set snares for me.

6. I say to the Lord, You are my God; give ear to the voice of my pleas for mercy, O Lord!

7. O Lord, my Lord, the strength of my salvation, you have covered my head in the day of battle.

8. Grant not, O Lord, the desires of the wicked; do not further their evil plot, or they will be exalted!

9. As for the head of those who surround me, let the mischief of their lips overwhelm them!

10. Let burning coals fall upon them! Let them be cast into fire, into miry pits, no more to rise!

11. Let not the slanderer be established in the land; let evil hunt down the violent man speedily!

12. I know that the Lord will maintain the cause of the afflicted, and will execute justice for the needy.

13. Surely the righteous shall give thanks to your name; the upright shall dwell in your presence.

Commentary

Psalm 140 is a plea for deliverance and protection from evil and violent people. The psalmist seeks God's intervention against those who plan evil, spread falsehoods, and set traps. They affirm their trust in the Lord and call upon Him to hear their pleas for mercy.

The psalmist acknowledges the wickedness and violence of their enemies. They describe their adversaries as those who plan evil things in their hearts, stir up wars, and use their tongues as sharp as serpents, filled with venomous speech.

The psalmist implores the Lord to guard and preserve them from the hands of the wicked. They acknowledge the traps and snares that have been set for them by the arrogant. They declare their faith in the Lord, recognizing Him as their God and seeking His attentive ear to their pleas for mercy.

The psalmist praises the Lord as the strength of their salvation, acknowledging His protection in times of battle. They request that the Lord does not grant the desires of the wicked or further their evil plots. The psalmist prays for the downfall of their enemies, asking that their mischief overwhelms them and that they face the consequences of their actions.

The psalmist expresses confidence in the Lord's justice and assurance that He will maintain the cause of the afflicted and execute justice for the needy. They anticipate that the righteous will give thanks to the Lord's name, and the upright will dwell in His presence.

Psalm 140 is a prayer for deliverance and protection from evil and violent individuals. The psalmist appeals to God to intervene on their behalf and to bring justice to those who plan and perpetrate harm. The psalmist expresses trust in God's ability to maintain the cause of the afflicted and affirms the ultimate victory of the righteous.

Psalm 122

2. Our feet have been standing within your gates, O Jerusalem!

6. Pray for the peace of Jerusalem! "May they be secure who love you!

9. For the sake of the house of the Lord our God, I will seek your good.

Commentary

Psalm 122 is a psalm of joy, celebration, and prayer for Jerusalem. The psalmist expresses their delight at the invitation to go to the house of the Lord in Jerusalem and describes the significance of the city and its purpose as the center of worship and governance.

The psalmist begins by expressing their gladness upon hearing the invitation to go to the house of the Lord. They relish the opportunity to stand within the gates of Jerusalem, the city where God's presence was known to dwell.

The psalmist describes Jerusalem as a city that is firmly bound together. It is a place to which the tribes of the Lord go up, as was decreed for Israel, to give thanks to the name of the Lord. The city of Jerusalem holds a special place in the hearts of the people, as it is the designated location for worship and the center of the nation's religious life.

The psalmist acknowledges the significance of Jerusalem as the place where thrones of judgment were set, specifically referring to the thrones of the house of David. Jerusalem served as the seat of power and authority for the nation, where justice and governance were established.

The psalmist offers a prayer for the peace of Jerusalem, asking for security and well-being for the city and its inhabitants. They desire peace within the walls and security within the towers of Jerusalem. The psalmist extends their prayer for peace to their brothers and companions, wishing peace upon them as well.

The psalmist expresses their commitment to seeking the good of Jerusalem for the sake of the house of the Lord. They recognize the importance of Jerusalem as the dwelling place of God's presence and express their dedication to seeking its welfare and prosperity.

Psalm 122 captures the joy and reverence associated with Jerusalem, the house of the Lord. It conveys the psalmist's gladness at the opportunity to worship in Jerusalem and acknowledges the significance of the city as the center of religious and political life. The psalm concludes with a prayer for the peace and security of Jerusalem, reflecting the psalmist's commitment to seeking the well-being of the city and its people.

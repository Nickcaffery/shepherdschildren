Psalm 120

1. In my distress I called to the Lord, and he answered me.

2. Deliver me, O Lord, from lying lips, from a deceitful tongue.

3. What shall be given to you, and what more shall be done to you, you deceitful tongue?

4. A warrior's sharp arrows, with glowing coals of the broom tree!

5. Woe to me, that I sojourn in Meshech, that I dwell among the tents of Kedar!

6. Too long have I had my dwelling among those who hate peace.

7. I am for peace, but when I speak, they are for war!

Commentary

Psalm 120 is a psalm of lament and supplication. The psalmist expresses distress and seeks deliverance from those who speak lies and practice deceit. The psalmist longs for peace but finds themselves dwelling among people who hate peace and seek conflict.

The psalmist begins by recounting their experience of distress and how they called upon the Lord, who answered them. They appeal to the Lord to deliver them from lying lips and deceitful tongues, highlighting the harm caused by false and deceptive speech.

The psalmist then directs a rhetorical question to the deceitful tongue, questioning what should be given to it and what further harm should befall it. They respond with imagery of a warrior's sharp arrows and glowing coals of the broom tree, depicting the consequences that await those who engage in deceitful speech.

The psalmist expresses a sense of lament and discomfort with their current situation. They mention Meshech and the tents of Kedar, which likely represent foreign and hostile lands. The psalmist feels like a sojourner or resident in these places, expressing their longing for a different dwelling.

The psalmist laments that they have dwelled among people who hate peace for far too long. Despite their desire for peace, the psalmist faces opposition and hostility from those around them. They affirm their commitment to peace, but when they speak, those around them respond with a disposition for war.

Psalm 120 reflects the psalmist's distress and longing for deliverance from deceitful speech and a hostile environment. It conveys the psalmist's desire for peace and their struggle with living among those who promote conflict. It serves as a prayer for deliverance and a plea for the Lord's intervention in the face of falsehood and hostility.

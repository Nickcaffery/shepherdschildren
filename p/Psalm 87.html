Psalm 87

1. On the holy mount stands the city he founded;

2. the Lord loves the gates of Zion more than all the dwelling places of Jacob.

3. Glorious things of you are spoken, O city of God. Selah

4. Among those who know me I mention Rahab and Babylon; behold, Philistia and Tyre, with Cushâ€” "This one was born there," they say.

5. And of Zion it shall be said, "This one and that one were born in her"; for the Most High himself will establish her.

6. The Lord records as he registers the peoples, "This one was born there." Selah

7. Singers and dancers alike say, "All my springs are in you."

Commentary

Psalm 87 celebrates the city of Zion, the dwelling place of God, and highlights the privilege and blessings associated with it.

The psalmist begins by emphasizing the significance of the holy mount, where the city of God, Zion, stands. They proclaim that the Lord loves the gates of Zion more than all the dwelling places of Jacob. This highlights the special favor and attention God bestows upon Zion.

The psalmist speaks of glorious things that are said about the city of God. The city is celebrated and honored by people from different nations and regions. The psalmist mentions Rahab (a symbolic name for Egypt) and Babylon, as well as Philistia, Tyre, and Cush. These nations recognize and acknowledge the greatness of Zion, even referring to it as the birthplace of its inhabitants. The psalmist affirms that the Most High Himself will establish Zion, indicating its divine foundation and significance.

The psalmist portrays God as the registrar of the peoples, recording and acknowledging those who are born in Zion. This highlights the spiritual and eternal significance of being associated with the city of God. The psalmist emphasizes the value and honor placed on those who are connected to Zion.

The psalm concludes with a declaration from singers and dancers who express their deep connection and joy in Zion. They proclaim that all their springs of life and blessings are found within the city of God.

Psalm 87 celebrates the unique and privileged status of the city of Zion as the dwelling place of God. It emphasizes the love and attention God has for Zion, as well as the recognition and honor given to it by nations and peoples. The psalm highlights the spiritual and eternal blessings associated with being connected to Zion. Singers and dancers express their joy and fulfillment in the city of God, recognizing it as the source of their abundant life.

Psalm 72

1. Give the king your justice, O God, and your righteousness to the royal son!

2. May he judge your people with righteousness, and your poor with justice!

3. Let the mountains bear prosperity for the people, and the hills, in righteousness!

4. May he defend the cause of the poor of the people, give deliverance to the children of the needy, and crush the oppressor!

5. May they fear you while the sun endures, and as long as the moon, throughout all generations!

6. May he be like rain that falls on the mown grass, like showers that water the earth!

7. In his days may the righteous flourish, and peace abound, till the moon be no more!

8. May he have dominion from sea to sea, and from the River to the ends of the earth!

9. May desert tribes bow down before him, and his enemies lick the dust!

10. May the kings of Tarshish and of the coastlands render him tribute; may the kings of Sheba and Seba bring gifts!

11. May all kings fall down before him, all nations serve him!

12. For he delivers the needy when he calls, the poor and him who has no helper.

13. He has pity on the weak and the needy, and saves the lives of the needy.

14. From oppression and violence he redeems their life, and precious is their blood in his sight.

15. Long may he live; may gold of Sheba be given to him! May prayer be made for him continually, and blessings invoked for him all the day!

16. May there be abundance of grain in the land; on the tops of the mountains may it wave; may its fruit be like Lebanon; and may people blossom in the cities like the grass of the field!

17. May his name endure forever, his fame continue as long as the sun! May people be blessed in him, all nations call him blessed!

18. Blessed be the Lord, the God of Israel, who alone does wondrous things.

19. Blessed be his glorious name forever; may the whole earth be filled with his glory! Amen and Amen!

The prayers of David, the son of Jesse, are ended.

Commentary

Psalm 72 is a royal psalm that expresses a prayer for the righteous and just rule of the king, asking for God's blessings and prosperity upon the king and the people. It highlights the attributes and responsibilities of a righteous ruler and concludes with praise to God.

The psalm begins by appealing to God to grant justice and righteousness to the king, referred to as the royal son. The prayer is for the king to judge the people with righteousness and the poor with justice. The psalmist desires prosperity and righteousness to flow like mountains and hills.

The psalmist prays for the king to defend the cause of the poor and needy, providing deliverance and crushing the oppressor. They express a desire for the king's reign to instill fear and reverence for God, lasting throughout generations. They use imagery of rain and showers watering the earth to symbolize the king's reign bringing refreshment and prosperity.

In the days of the righteous king, the psalmist prays for the flourishing of the righteous and the abundance of peace. They desire the king's dominion to extend from sea to sea and from the River to the ends of the earth. They anticipate desert tribes bowing down before the king and his enemies being humbled. The psalmist envisions tribute and gifts being rendered by the kings of Tarshish, the coastlands, Sheba, and Seba. They anticipate all kings and nations serving the righteous king.

The psalmist emphasizes the king's role in delivering the needy, the poor, and those without help. The king is characterized as having pity on the weak and saving the lives of the needy. The psalmist acknowledges the king's role in redeeming lives from oppression and violence, considering their blood precious in the king's sight.

The psalmist prays for the long life of the king, the giving of wealth and gifts, and continual prayer and blessings upon the king. They desire abundance and prosperity in the land, with the crops flourishing and the people thriving like the grass of the field. The psalmist prays for the enduring fame and blessedness of the king's name, with people from all nations calling him blessed.

The psalm concludes with a doxology, praising the Lord, the God of Israel, who alone does wondrous things. The psalmist blesses God's glorious name forever and desires the whole earth to be filled with His glory. The psalm concludes with an affirmation of these blessings with an "Amen and Amen."

Psalm 72 portrays the ideal of a righteous and just ruler who cares for the poor and needy. It expresses a prayer for God's blessings and prosperity upon the king and the people. The psalm acknowledges God's sovereignty and praises Him for His wondrous deeds.

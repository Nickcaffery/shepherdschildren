Psalm 138

1. I give you thanks, O Lord, with my whole heart; before the gods I sing your praise;

2. I bow down toward your holy temple and give thanks to your name for your steadfast love and your faithfulness, for you have exalted above all things your name and your word.

3. On the day I called, you answered me; my strength of soul you increased.

4. All the kings of the earth shall give you thanks, O Lord, for they have heard the words of your mouth,

5. and they shall sing of the ways of the Lord, for great is the glory of the Lord.

6. For though the Lord is high, he regards the lowly, but the haughty he knows from afar.

7. Though I walk in the midst of trouble, you preserve my life; you stretch out your hand against the wrath of my enemies, and your right hand delivers me.

8. The Lord will fulfill his purpose for me; your steadfast love, O Lord, endures forever. Do not forsake the work of your hands.

Commentary

Psalm 138 is a psalm of thanksgiving and praise to the Lord. It expresses the psalmist's gratitude for God's steadfast love, faithfulness, and deliverance. The psalmist acknowledges the exalted name and word of the Lord and declares His greatness.

The psalm begins with the psalmist offering thanks to the Lord with their whole heart and singing praises before the gods, likely referring to heavenly beings or rulers. The psalmist bows down towards the holy temple, recognizing the Lord's holiness and giving thanks for His steadfast love and faithfulness. The psalmist acknowledges that God has exalted His name and His word above all things.

The psalmist testifies to the Lord's faithfulness in answering their prayers and strengthening their soul. On the day the psalmist called out to the Lord, He answered and provided strength.

The psalmist declares that all the kings of the earth will give thanks to the Lord when they hear His words and witness His ways. The psalmist emphasizes the greatness of the Lord's glory. The psalmist further acknowledges the Lord's regard for the lowly while keeping the haughty at a distance.

Despite walking in the midst of trouble, the psalmist affirms that the Lord preserves their life. The Lord stretches out His hand against the psalmist's enemies and delivers them. The psalmist expresses confidence that the Lord will fulfill His purpose for them. They acknowledge the enduring nature of the Lord's steadfast love and request that He not forsake the work of His hands.

Psalm 138 is a psalm that expresses gratitude and praise to the Lord for His steadfast love, faithfulness, and deliverance. The psalmist recognizes the exalted name and word of the Lord and acknowledges His greatness. The psalmist testifies to the Lord's answering of prayers and strengthening of the soul. Despite facing trouble, the psalmist finds assurance in the Lord's preservation and deliverance. The psalm concludes with a plea for the Lord to continue His work and not forsake His creation.

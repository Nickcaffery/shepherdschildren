Psalm 1

1. Blessed is the one who does not walk in step with the wicked or stand in the way that sinners take or sit in the company of mockers,

2. but whose delight is in the law of the Lord, and who meditates on his law day and night.

3. That person is like a tree planted by streams of water, which yields its fruit in season and whose leaf does not witherâ€” whatever they do prospers.

4. Not so the wicked! They are like chaff that the wind blows away.

5. Therefore the wicked will not stand in the judgment, nor sinners in the assembly of the righteous.

6. For the Lord watches over the way of the righteous, but the way of the wicked leads to destruction.

Commentary

Psalm 1 explores the contrasting paths of the righteous and the wicked, emphasizing the blessings and prosperity that come from delighting in and meditating on the law of the Lord, as opposed to the ultimate destruction of the wicked.

Psalm 1 sets the tone for the entire Book of Psalms by presenting a contrast between the righteous and the wicked. The psalm offers insights into the characteristics, behaviors, and destinies of these two groups.

The psalm begins by declaring the blessedness of the person who avoids the influence of the wicked. They do not walk in step with them, stand in the path of sinners, or sit among those who mock God. Instead, their delight and joy come from meditating on the law of the Lord day and night. This portrays the righteous person's commitment to God's Word and their desire to align their thoughts and actions with His teachings.

The psalmist uses the metaphor of a tree planted by streams of water to describe the blessedness of the righteous. This tree flourishes, bearing fruit in its season and maintaining its vitality. Similarly, the righteous person who follows God's ways can expect spiritual growth, productivity, and stability in their life.

On the other hand, the wicked are compared to chaff, the worthless husks of grain that are easily blown away by the wind. Their way of life lacks substance and permanence. The psalm highlights the contrast between the righteous and the wicked, emphasizing that the righteous will prosper, while the wicked will face destruction.

The final verses reinforce this contrast by stating that the wicked will not stand in the judgment or be counted among the assembly of the righteous. The Lord watches over the way of the righteous, protecting and guiding them, while the way of the wicked leads to their downfall and ruin.

In summary, Psalm 1 encourages readers to choose the path of righteousness and delight in God's Word. It emphasizes the blessings and stability that come from following God's ways, contrasting them with the fleeting nature and ultimate destruction of the wicked.

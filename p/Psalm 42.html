Psalm 42

1. As a deer pants for flowing streams, so pants my soul for you, O God.

2. My soul thirsts for God, for the living God. When shall I come and appear before God?

3. My tears have been my food day and night, while they say to me all the day long, "Where is your God?"

4. These things I remember, as I pour out my soul: how I would go with the throng and lead them in procession to the house of God with glad shouts and songs of praise, a multitude keeping festival.

5. Why are you cast down, O my soul, and why are you in turmoil within me? Hope in God; for I shall again praise him, my salvation 6 and my God.

6. My soul is cast down within me; therefore I remember you from the land of Jordan and of Hermon, from Mount Mizar.

7. Deep calls to deep at the roar of your waterfalls; all your breakers and your waves have gone over me.

8. By day the Lord commands his steadfast love, and at night his song is with me, a prayer to the God of my life.

9. I say to God, my rock: "Why have you forgotten me? Why do I go mourning because of the oppression of the enemy?"

10. As with a deadly wound in my bones, my adversaries taunt me, while they say to me all the day long, "Where is your God?"

11. Why are you cast down, O my soul, and why are you in turmoil within me? Hope in God; for I shall again praise him, my salvation and my God.

Commentary

Psalm 42 expresses a deep longing for God and a sense of spiritual thirst. The psalmist's soul pants for God as a deer pants for flowing streams. They express a deep longing to come into the presence of God. The psalmist's tears have been their food day and night, while others mockingly ask them, "Where is your God?"

The psalmist reminisces about times of worship and fellowship in the house of God, leading the throng with glad shouts and songs of praise. They encourage themselves to hope in God, despite their current state of being cast down and in turmoil. They express confidence that they will again praise God, their salvation.

The psalmist acknowledges their current state of distress and remembers God from the land of Jordan, Hermon, and Mount Mizar. They describe a deep spiritual experience, where the call of their soul reaches out to the depths of God's greatness, comparing it to the roar of waterfalls and the crashing waves.

The psalmist acknowledges God's steadfast love during the day and expresses that His song is with them at night, serving as a prayer to the God of their life. They cry out to God, their rock, questioning why they feel forgotten and why they mourn because of the oppression of the enemy. The adversaries taunt them, repeating the mocking question, "Where is your God?"

In conclusion, the psalmist repeats the refrain of questioning their troubled and cast-down soul. They once again encourage themselves to hope in God, expressing confidence that they will again praise Him, their salvation and their God.

Psalm 42 reflects a deep longing for God's presence and an intense spiritual thirst. It portrays the psalmist's struggle with distress and the mocking of their adversaries. Despite their anguish, the psalmist finds solace in remembering God and places their hope in Him, trusting that they will once again praise Him. The psalm highlights the importance of seeking God's presence and finding hope and comfort in Him, even during times of turmoil.

Psalm 118

1. Give thanks to the Lord, for he is good; his steadfast love endures forever!

2. Let Israel say, "His steadfast love endures forever."

3. Let the house of Aaron say, "His steadfast love endures forever."

4. Let those who fear the Lord say, "His steadfast love endures forever."

5. Out of my distress, I called on the Lord; the Lord answered me and set me free.

6. The Lord is on my side; I will not fear. What can man do to me?

7. The Lord is on my side as my helper; I shall look in triumph on those who hate me.

8. It is better to take refuge in the Lord than to trust in man.

9. It is better to take refuge in the Lord than to trust in princes.

10. All nations surrounded me; in the name of the Lord, I cut them off!

11. They surrounded me, surrounded me on every side; in the name of the Lord, I cut them off!

12. They surrounded me like bees; they went out like a fire among thorns; in the name of the Lord, I cut them off!

13. I was pushed hard, so that I was falling, but the Lord helped me.

14. The Lord is my strength and my song; he has become my salvation.

15. Glad songs of salvation are in the tents of the righteous: "The right hand of the Lord does valiantly,

16. the right hand of the Lord exalts, the right hand of the Lord does valiantly!"

17. I shall not die, but I shall live, and recount the deeds of the Lord.

18. The Lord has disciplined me severely, but he has not given me over to death.

19. Open to me the gates of righteousness, that I may enter through them and give thanks to the Lord.

20. This is the gate of the Lord; the righteous shall enter through it.

21. I thank you that you have answered me and have become my salvation.

22. The stone that the builders rejected has become the cornerstone.

23. This is the Lord's doing; it is marvelous in our eyes.

24. This is the day that the Lord has made; let us rejoice and be glad in it.

25. Save us, we pray, O Lord! O Lord, we pray, give us success!

26. Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord! We bless you from the house of the Lord.

27. The Lord is God, and he has made his light to shine upon us. Bind the festal sacrifice with cords, up to the horns of the altar!

28. You are my God, and I will give thanks to you; you are my God; I will extol you.

29. Oh give thanks to the Lord, for he is good; for his steadfast love endures forever!

Commentary

Psalm 118 is a psalm of thanksgiving and celebration. It begins with a call to give thanks to the Lord for His goodness and enduring steadfast love. The psalmist calls upon different groups—Israel, the house of Aaron, and those who fear the Lord—to acknowledge and proclaim the Lord's steadfast love.

The psalmist shares their personal experience of distress and how they called upon the Lord, who answered and set them free. The psalmist confidently asserts that the Lord is on their side as their helper, and they will not fear what man can do to them. They express assurance that they will triumph over their enemies who hate them.

The psalmist extols the value of taking refuge in the Lord rather than trusting in man or princes. They describe how nations surrounded them, but in the name of the Lord, they were cut off and defeated. The psalmist uses vivid imagery to portray the enemies' futile efforts, comparing them to bees and a fire among thorns.

The psalmist testifies to the Lord's help in their time of trouble and declares that the Lord is their strength, song, and salvation. They express joy and gladness in the salvation that the Lord has brought. The psalmist rejoices in the righteousness and power of the Lord's right hand.

The psalmist affirms that they will not die but live, and they will proclaim the deeds of the Lord. They acknowledge the Lord's discipline, which has not resulted in their destruction but rather in their deliverance.

The psalmist requests the gates of righteousness to be opened to them, so they can enter and give thanks to the Lord. They recognize the significance of this gate as the way for the righteous to enter and worship the Lord. The psalmist praises the Lord for answering their prayers and becoming their salvation. They declare the rejected stone becoming the cornerstone, recognizing it as the Lord's doing.

The psalmist acknowledges the day that the Lord has made and calls for rejoicing and gladness in it. They pray for the Lord's salvation and success, and they bless the one who comes in the name of the Lord.

The psalmist affirms the Lord as God and recognizes His light shining upon them. They offer sacrifices and give thanks to the Lord, extolling His goodness and enduring steadfast love.

Psalm 118 serves as a testament to the psalmist's personal experience of the Lord's deliverance and salvation. It is a song of praise and thanksgiving, affirming the Lord's goodness, steadfast love, and faithfulness. The psalmist encourages others to join in giving thanks and acknowledging the Lord's enduring love.
